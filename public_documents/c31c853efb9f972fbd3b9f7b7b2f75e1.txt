Penalty of termination from\nservice would result in denial of pension and pensionary benefits to him in\nrespect of long service rendered by him. The point that\n\narise for my consideration in these petitions is &quot;whether\n\nthe complaint lodged by respondent No.2 and the F.I.R.\n\nregistered by respondent No.1 on the reference made by\n\nthe   Jurisdictional      Magistrate    and    the   proceedings\n\nthereon are liable to be quashed by this Court in\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_26\">                            : 25 :</span>\n\n\nexercise of inherent power under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_55\">Section 482</a> of the\n\nCode of Criminal Procedure.&quot;\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_17\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_18\">      12.    The law as to the circumstance under which\n\nthe High Court in exercise of the inherent power saved\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_56\">Section 482</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure\n\ncould quash the complaint and F.I.R. is now very much\n\ncrystallized. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1033637\" id=\"span_27\">In <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_57\">State of Haryana and Others vs. Ch.\n\nBhajan Lal and Others</a>, AIR 1992 SC 604, the Apex\n\nCourt has set out the categories of cases in which the\n\nHigh Court may exercise the power under <a href=\"/doc/1712542/\" id=\"a_58\">article 226</a> of\n\nthe constitution of India under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_59\">Section 482</a> of the Code\n\nof Criminal Procedure and interfere in proceedings\n\nrelating to cognizable offence to prevent abuse of\n\nprocess of any Court or otherwise to secure the ends of\n\njustice.    In this decision, the Apex Court has given a\n\nnote of caution that power should be exercised sparingly\n\nthat too in rarest of rare cases. The categories of cases\n\nset out in <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_60\">the said decision</a> which are relevant for our\n\npurpose are as under: -</span>\n</p><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_28\">                      : 26 :</span><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_19\">\n\n\n\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_1\">&quot;1)   Where the allegations made in the First\n      Information Report or the complaint,\n      even if they are taken at their face\n      value and accepted in their entirety do\n      not prima facie constitute any offence\n      or   make   out   a     case   against    the\n      accused.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_2\">2)    Where the allegations in the First\n      Information Report and the materials,\n      if any, accompanying the F.I.R. do not\n      disclose a cognizable offence, justifying\n      an   investigation      by   police   officers\n      under<a href=\"/doc/1376656/\" id=\"a_61\"> S.155(2)</a> of the Code.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_3\">3)    Where the uncontroverted allegations\n      made in the F.I.R. or complaint and\n      the evidence collected in support of the\n      same do not disclose the commission of\n      any offence and make out a case\n      against the accused.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_4\">5)    Where the allegations made in the\n      F.I.R. or complaint are so absurd and\n      inherently improbable on the basis of\n      which no prudent person can ever\n      reach a just conclusion that there is\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_29\">                             : 27 :</span>\n\n\n           sufficient     ground       for   proceeding\n           against the accused.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_5\">     7)    Where     a    criminal      proceeding   is\n           manifestly attended with mala fide\n           and/or       where    the    proceeding   is\n           maliciously instituted with an ulterior\n           motive for wreaking vengeance on the\n           accused and with a view to spite him\n           due to private and personal grudge.\n</blockquote>\n\n\n\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_20\">     13.   It is further ruled in this decision where\n\nallegations in the complaint did constitute a cognizable\n\noffence justifying registration of a case and investigation\n\nthereon and did not fall in any of the categories of cases\n\nenumerated therein calling for exercise of extraordinary\n\npower or inherent powers, quashing of F.I.R. is not\n\njustified. The aforesaid principles have been reiterated\n\nfrom time and again by the Apex Court in several\n\ndecisions. Keeping in mind the aforesaid principles, let\n\nme consider the case on hand.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_21\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_22\">     14.   As noticed supra, though in the complaint,\n\nthe complainant has alleged several offences punishable\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_30\">                               : 28 :</span>\n\n\nunder the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_62\">Indian Penal Code</a> in addition to the offences\n\npunishable under <a href=\"/doc/789969/\" id=\"a_63\">I.T. Act</a>, as could be seen from the\n\nF.I.R.,   respondent   No.1     Cyber   Crime   Police   have\n\nregistered the F.I.R. only for the offences punishable\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/711469/\" id=\"a_64\">Sections 66</a> and <a href=\"/doc/711469/\" id=\"a_65\">66(b)</a> of the IT Act.           This is\n\nobviously because the main grievance made out by the\n\ncomplainant is that its email account has been hacked\n\nby someone at the behest of these petitioners and they\n\nhave stolen the confidential informations and datas\n\navailable therein which have been used in other\n\nproceedings initiated by accused No.1. Thus, from the\n\nperusal of the F.I.R., it is clear that the investigation\n\ncommenced by the 1st respondent is only in relation to\n\nthe offence under the <a href=\"/doc/789969/\" id=\"a_66\">I.T. Act</a>.         However, if during\n\ninvestigation commission of any other offence which are\n\npunishable either under the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_67\">Indian Penal Code</a> or under\n\nany other law is revealed, the Investigation Officer may\n\ninclude such offence. However, whole case revolves on\n\nthe allegation of hacking into the email account of the\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_31\">                            : 29 :</span>\n\n\ncomplainant     and    stealing     of   the   confidential\n\ninformations.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_23\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_24\">     15.   No doubt, in the complaint, the complainant\n\nhas alleged that it believes some person at the behest of\n\naccused Nos.1 and 2 have illegally hacked its email\n\naccount and have gained unauthorised access to data\n\nand the informations of the complainant - Company\n\nand have stolen all such informations.         As noticed\n\nsupra, it is the contention raised on behalf of petitioners\n\nthat the said email was sent by the complainant -\n\nCompany itself by way of reply to the legal notice to\n\naccused No.2. The fact that the two emails referred to\n\nin the complaint originating from the email account of\n\nthe complainant - Company having been received by\n\naccused No.2 through his email account is not in\n\nserious dispute. Therefore, it has to be found out by a\n\nproper investigation as to who and from where sent the\n\nemails.    This can be found out only by a proper\n\ninvestigation by a specialised agency. Having regard to\n\nthe nature of the acts alleged, it cannot be possible for\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_32\">                                : 30 :</span>\n\n\nthe   complainant       to   make           definite   and    specific\n\nallegations against anyone. However, the fact that those\n\ninformations received by accused No.2 have been used\n\nby accused No.1 in various proceedings initiated by it\n\nagainst the complainant is not also seriously disputed.\n\nIt is not the case of the petitioners that those\n\ninformations were in public domain. Having regard to\n\nthe   fact    that   those   informations          relating   to   the\n\ncomplainant&#x27;s        Company      which         according     to   the\n\ncomplainant are confidential in nature, have been made\n\nuse   of     by   accused    No.1       -   Company     in    various\n\nproceedings initiated by it against the complainant -\n\nCompany, the complainant has alleged that it believes\n\nthat it was only at the behest of accused Nos.1 and its\n\nfunctionaries someone has hacked into the email\n\naccount and have stolen the informations.\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_25\">      16.     It is in this background, the argument of the\n\nlearned senior counsel regarding vicarious liability on\n\nthe part of the functionaries in the Company requires to\n\nbe considered.\n</p><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_33\">                              : 31 :</span><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_26\">\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_27\">       17.    No   doubt,   the   Apex   Court   in   various\n\ndecisions while interpreting the scope of <a href=\"/doc/686130/\" id=\"a_68\">Section 141</a> of\n\nthe Negotiable Instruments Act has held that it is\n\nnecessary for the complainant in such complaints to\n\nspecifically plead as to how the functionaries of the\n\nCompany are vicarious liable for the acts of the\n\ncompany. In Datar Switchgear Limited case, referred to\n\nsupra relied upon by the learned senior counsel for the\n\npetitioners, the Apex Court has held that there should\n\nbe specific averments in the complaint demonstrating\n\nthe role of the Chairman of Company when the offence\n\nalleged was by a Company. In this decision, it has been\n\nheld   that   presumption     cannot     be   drawn   that   a\n\nChairman of a Company is responsible for all acts\n\ncommitted by or on behalf of Company.            It is further\n\nheld that wherever, by a legal fiction principles of\n\nvicarious liability is attracted, it has to be specifically\n\nprovided in the statute concerned and it is incumbent\n\non the complainant to specifically aver the role of each\n\nof the accused in the complaint. <a href=\"/doc/1814177/\" id=\"a_69\">Section 85</a> of the I.T.\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_34\">                            : 32 :</span>\n\n\nAct, no doubt, is similar to the provisions of <a href=\"/doc/686130/\" id=\"a_70\">section 141</a>\n\nof the Negotiable Instruments Act.       However, having\n\nregard to the nature of the acts alleged and since the\n\ncomplainant by itself cannot ascertain as to who hacked\n\ninto its email account and stolen the informations and\n\nsince the belief of the complainant that such hacking\n\nhas been done at the behest of the accused persons is\n\nbased on the fact that such informations have been\n\nreferred to and used by accused No.1 in the various\n\nproceedings initiated by it against the complainant and\n\nsince the fact as to who hacked into the email account.\n\nIf there was a hacking, and whether to such hacking\n\nwas done at the behest of these petitioners are matters\n\nfor investigation by specialized agency, the analogy\n\ndrawn on the principles laid down in the aforesaid\n\ndecisions cannot be applied to the case on hand.\n\nHaving regard to the peculiar situation and since the\n\ncomplainant by itself cannot ascertain as to who hacked\n\ninto the email account, the complainant cannot be\n\nexpected to plead specific role of each of the petitioners.\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_35\">                             : 33 :</span>\n\n\nHaving regard to the fact that there undisputably those\n\ninformations have been used by accused No.1 in various\n\nproceedings initiated by accused no.1 against the\n\ncomplainant, want of averments regarding the specific\n\nrole of each of the petitioners cannot be a circumstance\n\nto quash the complaint and the F.I.R.            No doubt,\n\npresent complaint came to be filed after the complainant\n\nreceived notices in the various proceedings initiated\n\nagainst it by accused No.1. However, on that ground, it\n\ncannot be said that the complaint filed was with a\n\nmalafide intention of either coercing the petitioners or to\n\nwreak vengeance.     According to the complainant, he\n\ncame to know about the access to its email account by\n\nsomeone    only   after   receiving   the   notices   of   the\n\nproceedings initiated by accused No.1 before various\n\nForums.     Therefore, pendency of the proceedings\n\ninitiated by accused No.1 against the complainant do\n\nnot constitute a ground for quashing the compliant and\n\nthe F.I.R. Having regard to the allegations made in the\n\ncomplaint, it cannot be said that the offences alleged are\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_36\">                                : 34 :</span>\n\n\ncivil in nature, nor it can be said that the allegations\n\nmade in the complaint read as a whole do not constitute\n\nany offence.    As already noticed supra, the fact that\n\naccused No.2 received the email from the email account\n\nof the complainant is not in dispute. The fact that those\n\ninformations have been used in various proceedings\n\ninitiated by the accused No.1 against the complainant is\n\nalso admitted. No doubt, the petitioners contended that\n\nthese   two    emails   were     sent   by   the   complainant\n\nthemselves.    However, on the basis of such statement\n\nthis Court cannot quash the complaint or the F.I.R. in\n\nexercise of the inherent power saved under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_71\">Section 482</a>\n\nof the Code of Criminal Procedure. This is a disputed\n\nfact. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"460470\" id=\"span_37\">No doubt, in <a href=\"/doc/460470/\" id=\"a_72\">Anita Malhotra vs. Apparel Export\n\nPromotion Council and Another</a>, (2012) 1 SCC 520,\n\nthe Apex Court has held that though it is not proper for\n\nthe High Court to consider the defence of the accused or\n\nconduct a roving enquiry in respect of merits of the\n\naccusation, but if on the face of the document, which is\n\nbeyond suspicion or doubt, placed on record by the\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_38\">                           : 35 :</span>\n\n\naccused and if it is considered, the accusation against\n\nher cannot stand, in such a matter, in order to prevent\n\ninjustice or abuse of process, it is incumbent on the\n\nHigh Court to look into those document/documents\n\nwhich have a bearing on the matter, even at the initial\n\nstage and grant relief to the person concerned by\n\nexercising jurisdiction under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_73\">Section 482</a> of the Code of\n\nCriminal Procedure.    However, in the case on hand,\n\nsince the contention of the petitioners that the emails\n\nwere sent by the complainant themselves is a disputed\n\nfact and not borne out by any document beyond\n\nsuspicion or doubt, the aforesaid decision has no\n\napplication to the case on hand. Therefore, it cannot be\n\nsaid that no case is made out against the petitioners.</span>\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_28\">     18.
