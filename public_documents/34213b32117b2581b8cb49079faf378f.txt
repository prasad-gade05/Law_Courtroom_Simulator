I have heard the rival submissions.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_11\">ANALYSIS AND FINDINGS\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"RespArg\" id=\"p_12\">9.     The submission of the defendants that the defendant no.1, Google\nLLC uses the aforesaid warnings in respect of all third-party applications\nthat are downloaded from the internet has not disputed by the plaintiff.\nTherefore, the admitted position is that the said warning is in respect of all\nsuch file/application downloads and not confined to the plaintiff&#x27;s\napplication, and is not discriminatory.\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_13\">10.    The warning given by the defendants is in the nature of a disclaimer\nand does not prohibit or block the download. The users can continue to\ndownload and install the APK files by clicking on the option of &#x27;Download\nanyway&#x27;. It may be noted that APK files/applications like that of the plaintiff\nare not part of the &#x27;Google Play&#x27; ecosystem and therefore, the same do not\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_10\">                                                              Signature Not Verified</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_11\">                                                              Digitally Signed By:AMIT</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_12\">                                                              BANSAL</span>\n\nCS(COMM) 176/2022                                             Page\n                                                              Signing 4 of 11\n                                                                      Date:16.02.2023 16:57:47\n                               2023/DHC/001117\n\n\n\n\nundergo the various security checks and measures. Therefore, the defendants\nare only cautioning the user before the user proceeds to download the\napplication.\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_14\">11.   The defendants have also provided details that such warnings are not\nunique to the Google Chrome browser of the defendant no.1. Several other\nbrowsers also display such warning when viewers/potential users download\nthird-party APK files/applications from their websites. On a prima facie\nview, this appears to be the industry practice.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_15\">12.   In terms of the prevailing legal regime, the defendants are required to\nput in place such warnings so as to guard the user against potential threats.\nIn this regard, reference may be made to Rules 3(1)(i) and 3(1)(k) of the\nInformation Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics\nCode) Rules, 2021 [hereinafter &#x27;2021 IT Rules&#x27;] as well as Rule 8 of The\nInformation Technology (Reasonable Security Practices And Procedures\nAnd Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 [hereinafter &#x27;2011\nSecurity Rules&#x27;].\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_16\">13. The audit of reasonable security practices and\n      procedures shall be carried cut by an auditor at least once a\n      year or as and when the body corporate or a person on its\n      behalf undertake significant upgradation of its process and\n      computer resource.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"RespArg\" id=\"p_18\">15.   The counsel for the plaintiff has vehemently contended that the\ntrademark of the plaintiff is infringed/tarnished by the defendants when\nnaming the APK file/application &#x27;WinZO&#x27; in the warning displayed for its\nusers. At this stage, reference may be made to the relevant provisions of\n<a href=\"/doc/114322047/\" id=\"a_2\">Section 29</a>, which are set out below:\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_19\">      &quot;29. Infringement of registered trade marks.--(1) A\n      registered trade mark is infringed by a person who, not being a\n      registered proprietor or a person using by way of permitted\n      use, uses in the course of trade, a mark which is identical with,\n      or deceptively similar to, the trade mark in relation to goods or\n      services in respect of which the trade mark is registered and in\n      such manner as to render the use of the mark likely to be\n      taken as being used as a trade mark.\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_20\">      ...\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_21\">      (4) A registered trade mark is infringed by a person who, not\n      being a registered proprietor or a person using by way of\n      permitted use, uses in the course of trade, a mark which--\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_22\">         (a) is identical with or similar to the registered trade mark;\n         and\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_23\">         (b) is used in relation to goods or services which are not\n         similar to those for which the trade mark is registered; and\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_24\">         (c) the registered trade mark has a reputation in India and\n         the use of the mark without due cause takes unfair\n         advantage of or is detrimental to, the distinctive character\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_19\">                                                         Signature Not Verified</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_20\">                                                         Digitally Signed By:AMIT</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_21\">                                                         BANSAL</span>\n\nCS(COMM) 176/2022                                        Page\n                                                         Signing 7 of 11\n                                                                 Date:16.02.2023 16:57:47\n                              2023/DHC/001117\n\n\n\n\n          or repute of the registered trade mark.\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_25\">      ...\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_26\">      (6) For the purposes of this section, a person uses a registered\n      mark, if, in particular, he--\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_27\">         (a) affixes it to goods or the packaging thereof;\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_28\">         (b)offers or exposes goods for sale, puts them on the\n         market, or stocks them for those purposes under the\n         registered trade mark, or offers or supplies services under\n         the registered trade mark;\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_29\">         (c) imports or exports goods under the mark; or\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_30\">         (d) uses the registered trade mark on business papers or in\n         advertising.\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_31\">      ...\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_32\">      (8) A registered trade mark is infringed by any advertising of\n      that trade mark if such advertising--\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_33\">         (a) takes unfair advantage of and is contrary to honest\n         practices in industrial or commercial matters; or\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_34\">         (b) is detrimental to its distinctive character; or\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_35\">         (c) is against the reputation of the trade mark.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"RespArg\" id=\"p_19\">16.   There is merit in the submission of the defendants that the use of the\nplaintiff&#x27;s trademark in the aforesaid warning shall not constitute as a &#x27;mark\nlikely to be taken as being used as a trade mark&#x27; in terms of <a href=\"/doc/1377106/\" id=\"a_3\">Section 29(1)</a> of\nthe Trade Marks Act, 1999. Further, a perusal of <a href=\"/doc/297137/\" id=\"a_4\">Section 29(6)</a> of the Trade\nMarks Act, 1999 would show that the use of the impugned marks by the\ndefendants in their warning is not covered in any of the sub-clauses (a), (b),\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_20\">(c) or (d) of <a href=\"/doc/297137/\" id=\"a_5\">Section 29(6)</a>. A perusal of the warning would show that the\nreference to the name of the APK file/application &#x27;WinZO&#x27; is only for\nidentifying the file being downloaded for the purpose of the warning.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_21\">17.   It is a settled position of law that to make out a case for infringement\nunder <a href=\"/doc/774878/\" id=\"a_6\">Section 29(4)</a> of the Trade Marks Act, 1999, all three conditions under\nthe said provision have to be met. Since the defendant no.1, Google LLC is\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_22\">                                                          Signature Not Verified</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_23\">                                                          Digitally Signed By:AMIT</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_24\">                                                          BANSAL</span>\n\nCS(COMM) 176/2022                                         Page\n                                                          Signing 8 of 11\n                                                                  Date:16.02.2023 16:57:47\n                               2023/DHC/001117\n\n\n\n\nnot providing any goods or services using the impugned trademarks, the\ncondition in sub-clause (b) of <a href=\"/doc/774878/\" id=\"a_7\">Section 29(4)</a> is not satisfied. Hence, it does\nnot constitute &#x27;use of the trademark in the course of trade&#x27; within the\nmeaning of <a href=\"/doc/774878/\" id=\"a_8\">Section 29(4)</a>. Further, since the defendant no.1 is not\nadvertising goods/services by using the plaintiff&#x27;s marks in any manner,\nthere is no case made out for infringement under <a href=\"/doc/571997/\" id=\"a_9\">Section 29(8)</a> of the Trade\nMarks Act, 1999.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_22\">18.   Therefore, in my prima facie view, the reliance placed by the plaintiff\non <a href=\"/doc/84096/\" id=\"a_10\">Section 29</a> of the Trade Marks Act, 1999 to make out a case for\ninfringement/tarnishment of its &#x27;WinZO&#x27;/ &#x27;WinZO Games&#x27; marks, is\nmisplaced.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_23\">19.   As regards the ground of disparagement, indisputably, there is no\ncomparison between the products/services of the defendants with that of the\ngoods/services of the plaintiff. Nor is there any advertising for any goods or\nservices. Therefore, there is no competing interest of the products/services\nof the defendants involved and in my prima facie view, no case of\ndisparagement is made out.\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_24\">20.    Insofar as the ground of inducement of breach of contract between a\nuser and the plaintiff is concerned, the act of a user opting to download an\napplication from the website of the plaintiff would not result in a contract.\nAt best, a contract can come into place once the application is installed.\nSince there is no contract in place at the time the warning is displayed, there\ncannot be any question of inducement to breach the same. In fact, in\nparagraph 21 of the plaint, the plaintiff has admitted that when a user clicks\non the download link on the plaintiff&#x27;s website, the user is only &#x27;willing to\nexecute&#x27; a contract with the plaintiff. It is further stated that the warning\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_25\">                                                          Signature Not Verified</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_26\">                                                          Digitally Signed By:AMIT</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_27\">                                                          BANSAL</span>\n\nCS(COMM) 176/2022                                         Page\n                                                          Signing 9 of 11\n                                                                  Date:16.02.2023 16:57:47\n                               2023/DHC/001117\n\n\n\n\ndeters &#x27;willing&#x27; users from entering into a contract with the plaintiff.\nTherefore, there is no contract at the stage when the warning appears.\nParagraph 21 of the plaint is set out below:\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_25\">      &quot;21. It is further pertinent to note that when an internet user\n      searches &#x27;WinZO Games&#x27; on any search engine or clicks on\n      any paid/promoted links on any other social media platform or\n      other internet-based platform and accesses the Plaintiff&#x27;s\n      Website to download the Application, and ultimately clicks on\n      the download link on the Website, the said user is willing to\n      execute a contract with the Plaintiff by demonstrating a\n      willingness to download the Application. However, the Warning\n      displayed on Defendants&#x27; owned AOS device/platform deters\n      such willing users from executing the contract with the Plaintiff.\n      The Plaintiff, therefore, states that the Defendants&#x27; unwarranted\n      and baseless Warning amounts to tort of inducement for breach\n      of contract.&quot;\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_26\">21.    The submission that the warning of the defendants has resulted in\ndecline of downloads from the plaintiff&#x27;s website is also speculative. At this\nstage, the plaintiff is yet to establish a case on the aforesaid ground. This\naspect can only be established in the trial.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_27\">22.    Accordingly, I do not find any merit in the application and the same is\ndismissed.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_28\">23.
