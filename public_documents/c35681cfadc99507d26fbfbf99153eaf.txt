    "doc": "<h2 class=\"doc_title\">Section 499 in The Indian Penal Code, 1860</h2>\n\n<section class=\"akn-section\" id=\"section_499\"><h3>499. Defamation.\u2014</h3>\n<span class=\"akn-intro\"><span class=\"akn-p\">Whoever, by words either spoken or intended to be read, or by signs or by visible representations, makes or publishes any imputation concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing or having reason to believe that such imputation will harm, the reputation of such person, is said, except in the cases hereinafter expected, to defame that person.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation 1.\u2014 It may amount to defamation to impute anything to a deceased person, if the imputation would harm the reputation of that person if living, and is intended to be hurtful to the feelings of his family or other near relatives.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation 2.\u2014 It may amount to defamation to make an imputation concerning a company or an association or collection of persons as such.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation 3.\u2014 An imputation in the form of an alternative or expressed ironically, may amount to defamation.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation 4.\u2014 No imputation is said to harm a person\u2019s reputation, unless that imputation directly or indirectly, in the estimation of others, lowers the moral or intellectual character of that person, or lowers the character of that person in respect of his caste or of his calling, or lowers the credit of that person, or causes it to be believed that the body of that person is in a loathsome state, or in a state generally considered as disgraceful.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustrations</span></span><section class=\"akn-paragraph\" id=\"section_499.a\"><span class=\"akn-num\"><a href=\"/doc/715954/\">(a)</a></span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A says\u2014 \u201cZ is an honest man; he never stole B\u2019s watch\u201d; intending to cause it to be believed that Z did steal B\u2019s watch. This is defamation, unless it fall within one of the exceptions.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\" id=\"section_499.b\"><span class=\"akn-num\"><a href=\"/doc/861837/\">(b)</a></span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A is asked who stole B\u2019s watch. A points to Z, intending to cause it to be believed that Z stole B\u2019s watch. This is defamation unless it fall within one of the exceptions.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\" id=\"section_499.c\"><span class=\"akn-num\"><a href=\"/doc/766990/\">(c)</a></span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A draws a picture of Z running away with B\u2019s watch, intending it to be believed that Z stole B\u2019s watch. This is defamation, unless it fall within one of the exceptions.</span></span></section><span class=\"akn-hcontainer\" data-name=\"hcontainer\"><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">First Exception.\u2014 Imputation of truth which public good requires to be made or published.\u2014 It is not defamation to impute anything which is true concerning any person, if it be for the public good that the imputation should be made or published. Whether or not it is for the public good is a question of fact.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Second Exception.\u2014 Public conduct of public servants.\u2014 It is not defamation to express in a good faith any opinion whatever respecting the conduct of a public servant in the discharge of his public functions, or respecting his character, so far as his character appears in that conduct, and no further.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Third Exception.\u2014 Conduct of any person touching any public question.\u2014 It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion whatever respecting the conduct of any person touching any public question, and respecting his character, so far as his character appears in that conduct, and no further.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustration</span><span class=\"akn-p\">It is not defamation in A to express in good faith any opinion whatever respecting Z\u2019s conduct in petitioning Government on a public question, in signing a requisition for a meeting on a public question, in presiding or attending a such meeting, in forming or joining any society which invites the public support, in voting or canvassing for a particular candidate for any situation in the efficient discharges of the duties of which the public is interested.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Fourth Exception.\u2014 Publication of reports of proceedings of Courts.\u2014 It is not defamation to publish substantially true report of the proceedings of a Court of Justice, or of the result of any such proceedings.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation.\u2014 A Justice of the Peace or other officer holding an inquiry in open Court preliminary to a trial in a Court of Justice, is a Court within the meaning of the above section.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Fifth Exception.\u2014 Merits of case decided in Court or conduct of witnesses and others concerned.\u2014 It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion whatever respecting the merits of any case, civil or criminal, which has been decided by a Court of Justice, or respecting the conduct of any person as a party, witness or agent, in any such case, or respecting the character of such person, as far as his character appears in that conduct, and no further.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustrations</span></span></span><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(a)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A says\u2014\u201cI think Z\u2019s evidence on that trial is so contradictory that he must be stupid or dishonest\u201d. A is within this exception if he says this is in good faith, in as much as the opinion which he expresses respects Z\u2019s character as it appears in Z\u2019s conduct as a witness, and no further.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(b)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">But if A says\u2014\u201cI do not believe what Z asserted at that trial because I know him to be a man without veracity\u201d; A is not within this exception, in as much as the opinion which he express of Z\u2019s character, is an opinion not founded on Z\u2019s conduct as a witness.</span></span></section><span class=\"akn-hcontainer\" data-name=\"hcontainer\"><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">Sixth Exception.\u2014 Merits of public performance.\u2014 It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion respecting the merits of any performance which its author has submitted to the judgment of the public, or respecting the character of the author so far as his character appears in such performance, and no further.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Explanation.\u2014 A performance may be substituted to the judgment of the public expressly or by acts on the part of the author which imply such submission to the judgment of the public.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustrations</span></span></span><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(a)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A person who publishes a book, submits that book to the judgment of the public.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(b)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A person who makes a speech in public, submits that speech to the judgment of the public.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(c)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">An actor or singer who appears on a public stage, submits his acting or signing in the judgment of the public.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\" id=\"section_499.d\"><span class=\"akn-num\"><a href=\"/doc/516893/\">(d)</a></span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A says of a book published by Z\u2014 Z\u2019s book is foolish; Z must be a weak man. Z\u2019s book is indecent; Z must be a man of impure mind\u201d. A is within the exception, if he says this in good faith, in as much as the opinion which he expresses of Z respects Z\u2019s character only so far as it appears in Z\u2019s book, and no further.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\" id=\"section_499.e\"><span class=\"akn-num\"><a href=\"/doc/1440379/\">(e)</a></span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">But if A says\u2014 \u201cI am not surprised that Z\u2019s book is foolish and indecent, for he is a weak man and a libertine\u201d. A is not within this exception, in as much as the opinion which he expresses of Z\u2019s character is an opinion not founded on Z\u2019s book.</span></span></section><span class=\"akn-hcontainer\" data-name=\"hcontainer\"><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">Seventh Exception.\u2014 Censure passed in good faith by person having lawful authority over another.\u2014 It is not defamation in a person having over another any authority, either conferred by law or arising out of a lawful contract made with that other, to pass in good faith any censure on the conduct of that other in matters to which such lawful authority relates.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustration</span><span class=\"akn-p\">A Judge censuring in good faith the conduct of a witness, or of an officer of the Court; a head of a department censuring in good faith those who are under his orders; a parent censuring in good faith a child in the presence of other children; a school-master, whose authority is derived from a parent, censuring in good faith a pupil in the presence of other pupils; a master censuring a servant in good faith for remissness in service; a banker censuring in good faith the cashier of his bank for the conduct of such cashier as such cashier\u2014are within this exception.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Eighth Exception.\u2014 Accusation preferred in good faith to authorised person.\u2014 It is not defamation to prefer in good faith an accusation against any person to any of those who have lawful authority over that person with respect to the subject-matter of accusation.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustration</span><span class=\"akn-p\">If A in good faith accuse Z before a Magistrate; if A in good faith complains of the conduct of Z, a servant, to Z\u2019s master; if A in good faith complains of the conduct of Z, and child, to Z\u2019s father\u2014A is within this exception.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Ninth Exception.\u2014 Imputation made in good faith by person for protection of his or other\u2019s interests.\u2014 It is not defamation to make an imputation on the character of another provided that the imputation be made in good faith for the protection of the interests of the person making it, or of any other person, or for the public good.</span><span class=\"akn-p\">Illustrations</span></span></span><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(a)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A, a shopkeeper, says to B, who manages his business\u2014 \u201cSell nothing to Z unless he pays you ready money, for I have no opinion of his honesty\u201d. A is within the exception, if he has made this imputation on Z in good faith for the protection of his own interests.</span></span></section><section class=\"akn-paragraph\"><span class=\"akn-num\">(b)</span><span class=\"akn-content\"><span class=\"akn-p\">A, a Magistrate, in making a report of his own superior officer, casts an imputation on the character of Z. Here, if the imputation is made in good faith, and for the public good, A is within the exception.</span></span></section><span class=\"akn-wrapUp\"><span class=\"akn-p\">Tenth Exception.\u2014 Caution intended for good of person to whom conveyed or for public good.\u2014 It is not defamation to convey a caution, in good faith, to one person against another, provided that such caution be intended for the good of the person to whom it is conveyed, or of some person in whom that person is interested, or for the public good.</span></span></section>",
