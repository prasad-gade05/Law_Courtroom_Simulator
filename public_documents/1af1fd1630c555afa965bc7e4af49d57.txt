The jurisdiction of\n\n   Adjudicating Officer, under <a href=\"/doc/32503/\" id=\"a_28\">Section 43A</a>, read with <a href=\"/doc/1076139/\" id=\"a_29\">Section 46</a>, of\n\n   Information Technology Act, was restricted to claims involving :\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_1\">      (i)    body corporate possessing, handling and dealing in sensitive\n\n             personal data or information in a computer resource, which\n\n             it owns, controls or operates and\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_2\">      (ii)   negligence by such body corporate in implementing and\n\n             maintaining reasonable security practices and procedures (as\n\n             prescribed by <a href=\"/doc/76191164/\" id=\"a_30\">Section 43A</a> of Information Technology Act,\n\n             2000 read with Rule 8 of the Information Technology\n\n             (Reasonable security practices and procedures and sensitive\n\n             personal data or information) Rules, 2011, in respect of such\n\n             computer resource which owns, controls or operates.\n\n\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_23\">13.In the instant case, Appellant, being a Cellular Service provider, was\n\n   not handling the sensitive personal information, the unauthorized\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_11\">                                 15</span>\n\n\naccess of which caused the wrongful loss to Respondent No. 2, nor it\n\nwas negligent in implementing and maintaining reasonable security\n\npractices and procedures, as prescribed by <a href=\"/doc/32503/\" id=\"a_31\">Section 43A</a> of the Act,\n\nread with Rule 8 therein, in respect of any computer resource, which it\n\nowns, controls or operates. But, it was not appreciated by Ld.\n\nAdjudicating Officer. To bring a case in respect of <a href=\"/doc/32503/\" id=\"a_32\">Section 43A</a> of IT\n\nAct, Complainant must show that there is sensitive personal data or\n\ninformation, being possessed handled or dealt with by Appellant,\n\nwhereas, it was categorically stated that it does not possess, handle or\n\ndeal with any sensitive personal data or information. Therefore,\n\n<a href=\"/doc/32503/\" id=\"a_33\">Section 43A</a> of the Act was having with no obligation vis-\u00e0-vis cellular\n\ncompany Appellant is concerned. As per <a href=\"/doc/357830/\" id=\"a_34\">Indian Telegraph Act, 1885</a>,\n\n<a href=\"/doc/251935/\" id=\"a_35\">Indian Wireless Telegraphy Act, 1933</a> and as per Column 2 of the\n\nLicense Agreement, for provision of Unified Access Service, dated 6th\n\nNovember, 2011, granted by the Department of Telecom to Appellant,\n\nthe scope of the services, the Appellant is entitled to provide is\n\ncollection, carriage, transmission and delivery of voice and/or non-\n\nvoice messages over the Licensee&#x27;s network, in the designated Service\n\nArea. Hence, the role of Telecommunication Service Provider, is\n\nrestricted to providing access to its network, to the subscribers of such\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_12\">                                 16</span>\n\n\nTelecom Service Provider, to transmit messages in real time and other\n\nthan in relation to a transit nary period. Such messages are not saved\n\nor stored on any computer resources. The Appellant does not have\n\nany access to the content of the message (voice or text), being\n\ntransmitted over its network and it neither stores, nor possesses, nor\n\nhandles, nor deals with the contents of above messages, which are\n\nbeing transmitted over its network. Unless specifically being directed\n\nand instructed by Government of India, under <a href=\"/doc/1740460/\" id=\"a_36\">Section 5</a> of the Indian\n\nTelegraph Act, 1885, the Appellant has no access or right to intercept,\n\nin any manner, whatsoever, in respect of the messages or the content\n\nthereof, which are being transmitted on its network. Hence, question\n\nwith regard to sensitive personal data or information, never arises in\n\nthe course of Appellant&#x27;s function to provide the Services, under the\n\nLicense granted to it, by the Department of Telecommunications.\n\nThere were guidelines and directions, issued by Telecommunication\n\nDepartment, for observing, before issuing any mobile connection or\n\nits activation, in circular dated 9th August, 2012, which is Annexure -\n\n16 to the memo of Appeal. Accordingly, there is two tier mechanism,\n\nnamely,   Retailer/Franchisee    and   Distributor.   The   role   and\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_13\">                                    17</span>\n\n\n   responsibility of the Retailer/Franchisee and the Distributor are clearly\n\n   elucidated therein.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_24\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_25\">14. This all was adequately complied by Appellant. For seeking an order\n\n   of compensation, under <a href=\"/doc/394567/\" id=\"a_37\">Section 43</a> of the Act, the Complainant must\n\n   specifically identify the sensitive personal data or information, which\n\n   is possessed, handled or dealt with, by Cellular Telecom Company i.e.,\n\n   Appellant. But, it was not proved so. But Adjudicating Officer failed to\n\n   appreciate. On the contrary, Complainant had specifically admitted\n\n   that the only inconvenience caused to him, was the alleged non-\n\n   receipt of intimation of the transfer of money from alleged bank\n\n   account of complainant with bank - Respondent no. 4. In the light of\n\n   same, Adjudicating Officer had failed to bring out any finding on the\n\n   exact nature of sensitive personal data or information relating to\n\n   Appellant to cause alleged loss. The only personal data or information,\n\n   and the unauthorized access in Bank account, resulting wrongful loss\n\n   to complainant was the one time password communicated to\n\n   Complainant by bank.
