JUSTICE ANU MALHOTRA\n                   ORDER\n</pre><p data-structure=\"Issue\" id=\"p_1\">      %            04.06.2020\n\n      Vide the present petition, the petitioners 1 to 7 i.e. Rohan Duggal,\nAshwani Duggal, Anita Duggal, Pankaj Duggal, Daisy Duggal, Shweta Duggal\nand Rita Bhutani seek quashing of FIR No.216/2016, PS Chitaranjan Park\nregistered under <a href=\"/doc/538436/\" id=\"a_1\">Sections 498A</a>/<a href=\"/doc/988620/\" id=\"a_2\">406</a>/<a href=\"/doc/37788/\" id=\"a_3\">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 against\nthe petitioners No.1 to 7 and under <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_4\">Section 377</a> of the Indian Penal Code,1860\nagainst the petitioner No.1 Rohan Duggal submitting to the effect that a\nsettlement has since been arrived at between the parties vide a settlement deed\ndated 24.10.2019 and that no useful purpose would be served by the\ncontinuation of the proceedings in relation to the FIR in question.\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_2\">      The Investigating Officer of the case has identified the petitioners No.1 to\n7 present today in Court through video conferencing as being the seven accused\narrayed in the FIR in question and has also identified respondent no.2 present in\nCourt today through video conferencing as being the complainant of the said\n FIR.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Facts\" id=\"p_3\">       The respondent No.2 in reply to a specific Court query has testified to the\nsettlement having been arrived at between her and the petitioner No.1 on\n24.10.2019 and further submits that pursuant to the said settlement an amount of\nRs.9,00,000/- (Rs, Nine Lakhs) had been agreed to be paid to her by the\npetitioner No.1 of which the amount of Rs.8,00,000/- had since been received\nby her except Rs.1,00,000/- which has since been paid to her yesterday i.e.,\n3.6.2020 through RTGS. She thus submits that the entire settlement amount has\nsince been received by her and that there are now no claims of hers left against\nthe petitioners. The respondent No.2 has further testified to the effect that the\nmarriage between her and the petitioner No.1 has since been dissolved vide a\ndecree of divorce    dated 24.12.2019 through mutual consent in HMA No.\n1016/2019 of the Court of the Principal Judge, Family Court, South-East, Saket\nDelhi. The respondent No.2 further submits that thus in view of the settlement\narrived at between her and petitioners she does not oppose the prayer made by\nthe petitioners No. 1 to 7 seeking quashing of the FIR in question nor does she\nwant the petitioners to be punished in relation thereto. The respondent No.2\nfurther submits that she is a graduate and has done Masters in Multimedia and\nhas made her statement voluntarily of her own accord without any duress,\npressure or coercion from any quarter and after understanding the implications\nof the statement made by her.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_4\">       <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1803488\" id=\"span_1\">In view of the statement made by the respondent No.2 duly identified by\nthe Investigating Officer there appears no reason to disbelieve the statement of\nthe respondent no.2 that she has arrived at a settlement with the petitioners\nvoluntarily and she being a post graduate understands the implications of the\nstatement made by her, with their being no opposition on behalf of the State to\nthe prayer made by the petitioners seeking the quashing of the FIR in question,\nit is considered appropriate to put a quietus to the litigation and disputes\n between the parties for maintenance of peace and harmony between them as\nalso for the well being of the respondent no.2, in terms of the verdict of the\nHon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in <a href=\"/doc/1803488/\" id=\"a_5\">Narender Singh &amp; Ors. V. State of Punjab</a>; (2014)\n6 SCC 466 wherein it has been observed vide paragraph 31(IV) to the effect:</span>\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_1\">      &quot;31. In view of the aforesaid discussion, we sum up and lay\n      down the following principles by which the High Court\n      would be guided in giving adequate treatment to the\n      settlement between the parties and exercising its power\n      under <a href=\"/doc/903398/\" id=\"a_6\">Section 482</a> of the Code while accepting the settlement\n      and quashing the proceedings or refusing to accept the\n      settlement with direction to continue with the criminal\n      proceedings:\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_2\">      (I) ........\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_3\">      (II) ........\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_4\">      (III) ........\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_5\">      (IV) On the other, those criminal cases having\n      overwhelmingly and pre-dominantly civil character,\n      particularly those arising out of commercial transactions or\n      arising out of matrimonial relationship or family disputes\n      should be quashed when the parties have resolved their\n      entire disputes among themselves.\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_5\">      ...................&quot;\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_6\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"69949024\" id=\"span_2\">and in view of the observations of the Hon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in <a href=\"/doc/69949024/\" id=\"a_7\">Gian Singh\nvs. State of Punjab &amp; Another</a>, (2012) 10 SCC 303, to the effect : -</span>\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_6\">     &quot;58............................ No doubt, crimes are acts which have\n     harmful effect on the public and consist in wrongdoing that\n     seriously endangers and threatens the well-being of the society\n     and it is not safe to leave the crime-doer only because he and the\n     victim have settled the dispute amicably or that the victim has\n     been paid compensation, yet certain crimes have been made\n     compoundable in law, with or without the permission of the court.\n     In respect of serious offences like murder, rape, dacoity, etc., or\n     other offences of mental depravity under <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_8\">IPC</a> or offences of moral\n     turpitude under special statutes, like the <a href=\"/doc/1331755/\" id=\"a_9\">Prevention of Corruption\n     Act</a> or the offences committed by public servants while working in\n     that capacity, the settlement between the offender and the victim\n       can have no legal sanction at all. However, certain offences\n      which overwhelmingly and predominantly bear civil flavour\n      having arisen out of civil, mercantile, commercial, financial,\n      partnership or such like transactions or the offences arising out\n      of matrimony, particularly relating to dowry, etc. or the family\n      dispute, where the wrong is basically to the victim and the\n      offender and the victim have settled all disputes between them\n      amicably, irrespective of the fact that such offences have not\n      been made compoundable, the High Court may within the\n      framework of its inherent power, quash the criminal proceeding\n      or criminal complaint or FIR if it is satisfied that on the face of\n      such settlement, there is hardly any likelihood of the offender\n      being convicted and by not quashing the criminal proceedings,\n      justice shall be casualty and ends of justice shall be defeated.\n      The above list is illustrative and not exhaustive. Each case will\n      depend on its own facts and no hard-and-fast category can be\n      prescribed.&quot; [Refer to B.S. Joshi, (2003) 4 SCC 675; Nikhil\n      Merchant, (2008) 9 SCC 677 and Manoj Sharma, (2008) 16 SCC\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_7\">      1.]&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_7\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"127041521\" id=\"span_3\">and in view of the verdict of the Hon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in <a href=\"/doc/127041521/\" id=\"a_10\">Jitendra\nRaghuvanshi &amp; Ors. Vs. Babita Raghuvanshi &amp; Anr.</a> (2013) 4 SCC 58, to the\neffect : -</span>\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_8\">      &quot;15. In our view, it is the duty of the courts to encourage\n      genuine settlements of matrimonial disputes, particularly, when\n      the same are on considerable increase. Even if the offences are\n      non-compoundable, if they relate to matrimonial disputes and\n      the Court is satisfied that the parties have settled the same\n      amicably and without any pressure, we hold that for the purpose\n      of securing ends of justice, Section 320 of the Code would not be\n      a bar to the exercise of power of quashing of FIR, complaint or\n      the subsequent criminal proceedings.\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_9\">      16. There has been an outburst of matrimonial disputes in recent\n      times. They institution of marriage occupies an important place\n      and it has an important role to play in the society. Therefore,\n      every effort should be made in the interest of the individuals in\n      order to enable them to settle down in life and live peacefully. If\n      the parties ponder over their defaults and terminate their\n      disputes amicably by mutual agreement instead of fighting it out\n      in a court of law, in order to do complete justice in the\n      matrimonial matters, the courts should be less hesitant in\n      exercising their extraordinary jurisdiction. It is trite to state that\n     the power under Section 482 should be exercised sparingly and\n     with circumspection only when the Court is convinced, on the\n     basis of material on record, that allowing the proceedings to\n     continue would be an abuse of process of court or that the ends\n     of justice require that the proceedings ought to be quashed....&quot;\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Issue\" id=\"p_8\">                                                             <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"3376436\" id=\"span_4\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"5951139\" id=\"span_5\">(emphasis supplied),\n      As regards the allegations in the FIR qua the alleged commission of an\noffence punishable under <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_11\">Section 377</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 against\nthe petitioner No.1, in view of the verdict of this Court in Mayank Bansal &amp;\nOrs v. State &amp; Anr.vide Crl.M.C. No. 5290/2017 in as much as the allegation\nqua the alleged commission of offence punishable under <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_12\">Section 377</a> of the\nIndian Penal Code, 1860 relates to the petitioner No.1, the erstwhile spouse of\nthe respondent No.2 and which apparently allegedly occurred during the course\nof the matrimonial relation between them, and in view of the verdicts of this\nCourt in <a href=\"/doc/5951139/\" id=\"a_13\">Vineet Gupta &amp; Ors v. State &amp; Anr.</a>; Crl.M.C. No. 4891/2019, <a href=\"/doc/3376436/\" id=\"a_14\">Manav\nKhurana &amp; Ors. v. The State (NCT of Delhi) &amp; Anr.</a>; WP(Crl.)</span></span> <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"131161160\" id=\"span_6\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"114770195\" id=\"span_7\">No. 2241/2019\ndated 5.11.2019, <a href=\"/doc/114770195/\" id=\"a_15\">Ravinder Kumar &amp; Ors. v. The State (Govt. of NCT of Delhi)\n&amp; Anr. Crl.M.C. No.</a> 1891/2015 dated 18.8.2015, <a href=\"/doc/131161160/\" id=\"a_16\">Saurabh Gupta &amp; Ors. v.\nThe State (Govt. of NCT of Delhi) W.P.(Crl</a>).</span></span> No.2377/2018 dated 10.8.2018\nand in Shri Prateek Dhawan &amp; Ors. Vs. The State &amp; Anr. in Crl.M.C. No.\n2464/2019 dated 31.10.2019 and in Shri Manish Verma Vs. State &amp;\nAnr.Crl.M.C. 4227/2019 dated 31.10.2019, it is considered appropriate to put a\nquietus to the litigation between the parties even qua the alleged commission of\nthe offence punishable under <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_17\">Section 377</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860,\nalleged against the petitioner No.1 in relation to the FIR in question registered\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_18\">Section 377</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 which emanates apparently\nfrom the matrimonial discord between them which has since been set at rest\nbetween the petitioner No.1 and the respondent No.2 vide a decree of divorce\nthrough mutual consent, in these circumstances for maintenance of peace and\nharmony between the parties, thus the FIR No.216/2016, PS Chitaranjan Park\n registered under <a href=\"/doc/538436/\" id=\"a_19\">Sections 498A</a>/<a href=\"/doc/988620/\" id=\"a_20\">406</a>/<a href=\"/doc/37788/\" id=\"a_21\">34</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 against\nthe petitioners No.1 to 7 and under <a href=\"/doc/1836974/\" id=\"a_22\">Section 377</a> of the Indian Penal Code, 1860\nagainst the petitioner No.1 and all consequential proceedings emanating\ntherefrom are thus quashed.    The petition is disposed of.\n</p>\n\n\n\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_9\">                                                  ANU MALHOTRA, J\nJUNE 04, 2020\nSV\n IN THE HIGH COURT OF DELHI: NEW DELHI\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_8\">7</span>\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_9\">CRL.M.C. 1489/2020</span>\nROHAN DUGGAL &amp; ORS. I further state that there is no child born of the\nwedlock between me and the petitioner No.1 and I have received all my articles\nin terms of the settlement deed dated 24.10.2019.\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_13\">       I have done Masters in Multimedia and I have made my statement after\nunderstanding its implications voluntarily of my own accord without any\nduress, coercion or pressure from any quarter.\n</p>\n\n\n\n<pre id=\"pre_3\">RO &amp; AC                                          ANU MALHOTRA, J\n04.06.2020\n </pre>",
