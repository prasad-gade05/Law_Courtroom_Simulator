<span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"80997184\" id=\"span_3\">2. In these batch of writ petitions, several newspapers have\n\nchallenged the launching of prosecution of Criminal defamation against\n\nthem under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_13\">Section 499</a> IPC by the State Government through the Public\n\nProsecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_14\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. In the respective cases, either\n\nthe Government order sanctioning the prosecution is challenged or the\n\ncriminal complaint pending before the Sessions Judge is challenged or in\n\nsome cases, both the Government order and the criminal complaint are\n\nchallenged.    Some   of   the   petitioners   have   also     challenged        the\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_4\">4/153</span>\n\f                                                      W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nconstitutional validity of criminal defamation falling under chapter XXI\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_15\">IPC</a> (which comprises of <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_16\">Sections 499</a> to <a href=\"/doc/1495647/\" id=\"a_17\">502</a> IPC). But that issue has now\n\nbeen well settled by the Hon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in the case of\n\n<a href=\"/doc/80997184/\" id=\"a_18\">Subramaniam Swamy vs. Union of India</a> reported in (2016) 7 SCC 221\n\nas the Supreme Court has held the said section to be constitutionally\n\nvalid. Therefore, there is no necessity for this Court to give its ruling on\n\nthe constitutionality of criminal defamation.</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Issue\" id=\"p_13\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Issue\" id=\"p_14\">        3. In order to get answers to the questions raised in these batch\n\nof writ petitions, we need to examine and analyze the following:\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_27\">         A. Chapter XXI of the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_38\">Indian Penal Code</a> which deals with Criminal\n\ndefamation and comprises of sections 499 to 502.\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_28\">         B. Relevant decisions of the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court and other High\n\ncourts on the substantive and procedural aspects of Criminal defamation\n\nlaw.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_29\">         C. Criminal defamation law in other major Democracies as well as\n\nthe UN Convention on Tolerance, 1995.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_30\">         D. Opinion of jurists on Freedom of speech and expression under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1142233/\" id=\"a_39\">article 19 (1)</a> of the Constitution of India.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_31\">\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_84\">         12. Chapter XXI of the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_40\">Indian Penal Code</a> deals with Criminal\n\ndefamation law and comprises of 4 sections namely sections 499 to 502.\n\nSection 499 is the charging section for criminal defamation. Section 500\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_103\"><a href=\"/doc/1437597/\" id=\"a_41\">70</a>/<a href=\"/doc/1009986/\" id=\"a_42\">153</a></span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nis the punishment section for criminal defamation. Section 501 is the\n\ncharging and punishment section for printing or engraving matter known\n\nto be defamatory and section 502 is the charging and punishment section\n\nfor sale of printed or engraved a substance containing defamatory\n\nmatter. Therefore only when the alleged defamatory act comes within\n\nthe definition of <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_43\">section 499</a> IPC, sections 500 to 502 gets attracted.\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_85\">         13. We shall now examine and analyze the pedestal section of\n\nChapter XXI of <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_44\">IPC</a> namely <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_45\">section 499</a> which is the definition section.\n\nSection 499 defines defamation as:\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_32\">               \u201cWhenever, by words, either spoken or intended to be\n         read, or by signs or by visible representations, makes or\n         publishes any imputation concerning any person intending to\n         harm, or knowing or having reason to believe that such\n         imputation will harm, the reputation of such person, is said\n         to defame that person.\u201d\n\nTherefore the essence of criminal defamation is that the person charged\n\nfor the said offence must have the intention to harm the reputation of\n\nthe person against whom words have been spoken or any article has been\n\npublished by him.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_33\">\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_104\">71/153</span>\n\f                                                      W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_86\">         14. <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_46\">Section 499</a> provides for 10 exceptions which are as follows:\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_34\">         a) Imputation of truth which public good requires to be made or\n\npublished.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_35\">\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_36\">         b) Public conduct of public servants.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_37\">         c) Conduct of any person touching any public question.\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_38\">         d) Publication of reports of proceedings of courts.\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_39\">         e) Merits of a case decided in court or conduct of witnesses and\n\nothers concerned.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_40\">         f) Merits of public performance.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_41\">         g) Censure passed in good faith by person having lawful authority\n\nover another.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_42\">         h) Imputation made in good faith by person for protection of his or\n\nothers\u2019 interests.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_43\">         i) Caution intended for the good of person to whom conveyed or\n\nfor public good.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_44\">\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_87\">         15. <a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_47\">Section 500</a> is the punishment section for criminal defamation.\n\nThe section provides that the punishment for criminal defamation is\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_105\">72/153</span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nsimple imprisonment for a term which may extend to 2 years, or with\n\nfine, or with both.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_88\">\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_89\">         16. <a href=\"/doc/1820068/\" id=\"a_48\">Section 501</a> deals with the offence of criminal defamation\n\nrelating to printing or engraving matter known to be defamatory. The\n\npunishment for the said offence is the same as that of <a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_49\">section 500</a> IPC.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_90\">         17. <a href=\"/doc/1495647/\" id=\"a_50\">Section 502</a> deals with the offence of criminal defamation\n\nrelating to sale of printed or engraved substance containing defamatory\n\nmatter. The punishment for this offence is also the same as that of\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_51\">section 500</a> IPC.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_91\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_92\">         18. The offence of criminal defamation is a non-cognizable offence\n\nunder the <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_52\">Criminal Procedure Code</a>. Criminal defamation is the only non-\n\ncognizable offence in the entire <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_53\">Indian Penal Code</a> having a large number\n\nof exceptions to the offence. This will indicate that the intention of the\n\nlegislature is to restrict the usage of the criminal defamation law.\n\n\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_106\">73/153</span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Issue\" id=\"p_93\">         19. In all these batch of writ petitions, the petitioners are\n\nnewspapers and they have pleaded either a) truth b) not the author of\n\nthe article c) good faith d) public good e) public conduct f) conduct\n\ntouching public question in the articles published. Some of them have\n\npleaded all the above exceptions and some a few of them.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_94\">         20. <a href=\"/doc/739296/\" id=\"a_54\">Section 199</a> of the criminal Procedure Code prescribes the\n\nprocedure for prosecuting criminal defamation offences. There are six\n\nsubsections in <a href=\"/doc/739296/\" id=\"a_55\">section 199</a> and each of them are itself unique in its\n\napplication.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_95\">         (a) Subsection 1 provides that only an aggrieved person can launch\n\nprosecution for criminal defamation.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_96\">         (b) Subsection 2 provides for a special procedure in cases where\n\nthe imputation is made against the constitutional functionary/public\n\nservant in respect of his conduct in the discharge of his public functions.\n\nThis special procedure provides for filing of a complaint through a public\n\nprosecutor before the sessions court.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Facts\" id=\"p_97\">         (c) Subsection 3 narrates the facts which are required to be\n\npleaded in a complaint filed for criminal defamation under <a href=\"/doc/739296/\" id=\"a_56\">section 199(2)</a>\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_107\"><a href=\"/doc/1105025/\" id=\"a_57\">74</a>/<a href=\"/doc/857209/\" id=\"a_58\">153</a></span>\n\f                                                     W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n<a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_59\">Cr.P.C</a>. through a public prosecutor. It states that the complaint shall set\n\nforth in the facts which constitutes the offence alleged, the nature of\n\nsuch offence and such other particulars as our reasonably sufficient to\n\ngive notice to the accused of the offence alleged to have been\n\ncommitted by him. It is to be noted here that for no other non-\n\ncognizable offence in the entire Penal Code, there is a stipulation about\n\nthe factual requirements that are to be pleaded in a private complaint\n\nfor Criminal defamation filed under <a href=\"/doc/739296/\" id=\"a_60\">Section 199(2)</a>.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_98\">         (d) Subsection 4 provides that for filing a complaint by the public\n\nprosecutor under subsection 2, sanction of the State Government or the\n\nCentral Government as the case may be is mandatory.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_99\">         (e) Subsection 5 prescribes six months time limit for launching\n\nprosecution through a public prosecutor under subsection 2.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_100\">         (f) Subsection 6 is an omnibus provision enabling any aggrieved\n\nperson including public servant/constitutional functionary to launch\n\nprosecution for criminal defamation before the Magistrate dehors the\n\nspecial procedure available under subsection 2 in cases of imputation\n\nmade against a constitutional functionary/public servant to his conduct\n\nin the discharge of his public functions. It must be noted here that the\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_108\">75/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\npunishment provided for criminal defamation under the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_61\">Indian Penal\n\nCode</a> is same whether the prosecution is launched through the public\n\nprosecutor in the court of Sessions under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_62\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C. or by\n\nthe aggrieved personally under <a href=\"/doc/1459845/\" id=\"a_63\">section 199(6)</a> Cr.P.C. before the\n\nMagistrate.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_101\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_102\">         21. <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_64\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. also does not bar a constitutional\n\nfunctionary/public servant from personally launching prosecution for\n\ncriminal defamation before the Magistrate under <a href=\"/doc/1459845/\" id=\"a_65\">section 199(6)</a> Cr.P.C.\n\neven in cases of defamation in respect of his conduct in the discharge of\n\nhis public functions. The intention of the legislature would never have\n\nbeen to overlap the applicability of subsections within a section. The rule\n\nof harmonious construction comes into play. The rule falls on the premise\n\nthat every statute/section has a purpose of intent as per law and should\n\nbe meaningful. The rule of Harmonious construction is the thumb rule\n\nwhile interpreting any statute. The interpretation which makes the\n\nenactment consistent and the interpretation which avoids inconsistency\n\nor repugnancy should be the aim of the courts. Therefore there is an\n\nintelligible differentia between subsection (2) and subsection (6) of\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_109\">76/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n<a href=\"/doc/27007/\" id=\"a_66\">section 199</a>. Subsection (2) is the procedure for launching prosecution in\n\ncase of defamation against the State and subsection (6) is for personal\n\ndefamation even if it is a case of defamation against a public\n\nservant/constitutional functionary in the discharge of his public functions\n\nwhich are personal in nature and where state has not been defamed.\n\nPublic servant/constitutional authority is required to do selfless service\n\nto the State. The Legislature would never have intended to launch\n\nprosecution through a Public Prosecutor to serve the personal interest of\n\nthe public servant/constitutional authority alone, even if the said\n\ndefamation of the public servant/constitutional authority was made in\n\nthe discharge of his / her public functions. Unless, the element of the\n\nState also being defamed along with the public servant/constitutional\n\nauthority is satisfied, the question of launching prosecution through the\n\npublic prosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_67\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C will never arise as it\n\ninvolves a special procedure for criminal defamation against the State.\n\nThe subtle difference between <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_68\">Section 199(2)</a> and <a href=\"/doc/1459845/\" id=\"a_69\">199(6)</a> Cr.P.C is also\n\nsupported by decisions of the Honourable Supreme Court which will be\n\ndiscussed by this court in the forthcoming paragraphs of this common\n\nOrder.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_103\"><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_110\">77/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_104\">         22. <a href=\"/doc/1271595/\" id=\"a_70\">Section 24</a> of the Code of Criminal Procedure provides for\n\nappointment of public prosecutors. Public prosecutors are appointed by\n\nthe State or Central Government as the case may be to conduct in the\n\ncourt any prosecution, appeal or other proceeding on behalf of the\n\nCentral Government or State Government as the case may be. As seen\n\nfrom <a href=\"/doc/1271595/\" id=\"a_71\">section 24</a>, a public prosecutor can represent only the interest of\n\nthe State/Central Government and not any other person though he may\n\nbe a constitutional functionary/public servant. It must be noted here\n\nthat under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_72\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C., prosecution is launched through a\n\npublic prosecutor and hence, it has to be examined and analyzed as to\n\nwhether for a public prosecutor to file a complaint is it sufficient if the\n\ncriminal defamation has been committed against the constitutional\n\nfunctionary/public servant in respect of his conduct in the discharge of\n\nhis public functions or in addition to that requirement whether the state\n\nshould also have been defamed. The decisions of the Honourable\n\nSupreme Court and the reasonings of this court to be narrated in the\n\nforthcoming paragraphs will shed light and give answers to these\n\nquestions.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_105\"><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_111\">78/153</span>\n\f                                                     W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_106\">         23. Under <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_73\">section 200</a> Cr.P.C, a Magistrate taking cognizance of a\n\ncomplaint will have to examine upon oath the complainant and the\n\nwitnesses present, if any, and the substance of such examination shall be\n\nreduced into writing and shall be signed by the complainant and the\n\nwitnesses and also by the Magistrate. The exception to this procedure of\n\nexamining the complainant and witnesses is when the complainant is a\n\npublic servant acting or purporting to act in the discharge of his public\n\nduties or a court has made the complaint; or if the Magistrate makes\n\nover the case for enquiry or trial to another magistrate under <a href=\"/doc/414294/\" id=\"a_74\">section 192</a>\n\nCr.P.C. Therefore before taking cognizance of a private complaint under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_75\">section 200</a> Cr.P.C., it is mandatory to examine the complainant and the\n\nwitnesses present on oath. A complaint for criminal defamation filed\n\nthrough a public prosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_76\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. is also a private\n\ncomplaint though filed before the Sessions Court.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_107\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"321019\" id=\"span_112\">24. The proviso (a) to <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_77\">section 200</a> Cr.P.C. grants exemption only to\n\na public servant acting or purporting to act in the discharge of his official\n\nduties and does not grant exemption to a public prosecutor to be\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_113\">79/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nexamined by a Magistrate before taking cognizance of a complaint for\n\ncriminal defamation filed under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_78\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. Further, as hled\n\nby the Hon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in the case of <a href=\"/doc/321019/\" id=\"a_79\">State of U.P and others vs.\n\nJohri Mal</a>, reported in (2004) 4 SCC 714 in paragraph 39 that Public\n\nprosecutors retain the character of legal practitioners for all intent and\n\npurport. They of course, discharge public functions and certain statutory\n\npowers are also conferred on them. Their duties and function                 are\n\nonerous but the same would not mean that their condition of\n\nappointment are governed by any statute or statutory rule. Therefore, in\n\nthe considered view of this Court, by holding the post of public\n\nprosecutors, they cannot be exempted from giving evidence. As\n\nindependent application of mind is required by the public prosecutor, his\n\nexamination as a witness can never be dispensed with. None of the\n\nsubsections in <a href=\"/doc/27007/\" id=\"a_80\">section 199</a> Cr.P.C. also exempts the public prosecutor\n\nfrom being examined as mandatorily required under <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_81\">section 200</a> Cr.P.C.\n\nWhen the <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_82\">Criminal Procedure Code</a> makes it mandatory to examine the\n\ncomplainant on oath for a regular private complaint under <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_83\">section 200</a>,\n\nthe same mandatory requirement for a prosecution for criminal\n\ndefamation launched through a public prosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_84\">section 199(2)</a>\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_114\"><a href=\"/doc/563119/\" id=\"a_85\">80</a>/<a href=\"/doc/1009986/\" id=\"a_86\">153</a></span>\n\f                                                        W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n<a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_87\">Cr.P.C</a>. has to be necessarily applied as the level of scrutiny by the\n\nSessions Court is much higher in case of complaints filed for criminal\n\ndefamation through the public prosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_88\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C.\n\nThough the procedure for prosecution in <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_89\">Section 200</a> Cr.P.C talks about\n\nonly     Private   Complaints   before   Magistrates,     the     procedure        for\n\nexamination of Complainant contemplated in <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_90\">Section 200</a> Cr.P.C. will\n\nalso have to be necessarily imported for Complaints filed under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_91\">Section\n\n199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. before the Sessions Court, where the level of scrutiny is\n\nmuch higher.</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_108\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_109\">         25. Having examined the relevant provisions under the Indian Penal\n\nCode and the Criminal Procedure Code, this Court shall now consider the\n\ndecisions of the Honourable Supreme Court on\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_110\">         a) the law of Criminal Defamation\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_111\">         b) Role of the public prosecutor and his duties.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_112\">         c) Duties of the Magistrate /Sessions judge while taking cognizance\n\nof a private complaint.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_113\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_114\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"61358503\" id=\"span_115\">26. The Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in the case of Subramanian\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_116\">81/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n<a href=\"/doc/61358503/\" id=\"a_92\">Swamy Vs Union of India</a> reported in (2016) 7 SCC 221 though rejecting\n\nthe challenge to the constitutional validity of criminal defamation law\n\nhas however laid down the required parameters to launch prosecution for\n\ncriminal defamation under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_93\">section 499</a> IPC. The well laid down\n\nparameters are as follows:</span>\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_45\">         a) To constitute the offence of Defamation under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_94\">section 499</a> IPC,\n\nthere has to be imputation and it must have been made in the manner as\n\nprovided in the provision with the intention of causing harm or having\n\nreason to believe that such imputation will harm the reputation of a\n\nperson about whom it is made. Causing harm to the reputation of a\n\nperson is the basis on which the offence is founded and Mens rea is a\n\ncondition precedent to constitute the said offence. The complainant has\n\nto show that the accused had intended or known or had reason to believe\n\nthat the imputation made by him would harm the reputation of the\n\ncomplainant. The Criminal offence emphasizes on the intention of the\n\nharm. <a href=\"/doc/1106981/\" id=\"a_95\">Section 44</a> of the IPC defines injury. It denotes any harm whatever\n\nillegally caused to any person, in body, mind, reputation or property.\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_115\">Thus, the word injury encapsulates harm caused to the reputation of any\n\nperson. It also takes into account the harm caused to a person\u2019s body and\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_117\">82/153</span>\n\f                                                     W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nmind. Section 499 provides for harm caused to the reputation of a\n\nperson, that is, the complainant.( Para 168)\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_116\">         b) In the name of freedom of speech and expression, the right of\n\nanother cannot be jeopardized. Therefore, what is required is sustenance\n\nand balancing of the separate rights, one under <a href=\"/doc/1378441/\" id=\"a_96\">Article 19 (1) (a)</a> and the\n\nother under <a href=\"/doc/1199182/\" id=\"a_97\">Article 21.</a> It is not a case of mere better enjoyment of\n\nanother freedom. Balancing equipoise and counterweighing fundamental\n\nrights is a constitutional necessity. It is the duty of the court to strike a\n\nbalance so that the values are sustained.(Paras 144,136 &amp;137).\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_117\">         c) A studied scrutiny of the provisions makes it clear that a public\n\nservant is entitled to file a complaint through the public prosecutor in\n\nrespect of his conduct in discharge of his public functions. Public function\n\nstands on a different footing than the private activities of a public\n\nservant. The provision gives protection for their official acts. There\n\ncannot be defamatory attacks on them because of discharge of their due\n\nfunctions. In that sense, they constitute a different class. Be it clarified\n\nhere that criticism is different than defamation. One is bound to tolerate\n\ncriticism, dissent and discordance but not expected to tolerate\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_118\">83/153</span>\n\f                                                     W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ndefamatory attack.(Para 202)\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"PetArg\" id=\"p_118\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"115650339\" id=\"span_119\">d) The provision relating to engagement of the public prosecutor in\n\ndefamation cases in respect of the said authorities is seriously criticised\n\non the ground that it allows unnecessary room to the authorities\n\nmentioned therein and the public servants to utilize the public\n\nprosecutor to espouse their cause for vengeance. Once it is held that the\n\npublic servants constitute a different class in respect of the conduct\n\npertaining to their discharge of duties and functions, the engagement of\n\npublic prosecutor cannot be found fault with. It is ordinarily expected\n\nthat the public prosecutor has a duty to scan the materials on the basis\n\nof which a complaint for defamation is to be filed. He has a duty towards\n\nthe court. This court in by <a href=\"/doc/115650339/\" id=\"a_98\">Bairam Muralidhar Vs State of A.P</a> reported in\n\n(2014) 10 SCC 380 while deliberating on <a href=\"/doc/1037589/\" id=\"a_99\">section 321</a> Cr.P.C. has opined\n\nthat the public prosecutor cannot act like a post office on behalf of the\n\nState Government. He is required to act in good faith, peruse the\n\nmaterials on record and form an independent opinion. It further observed\n\nthat he cannot remain oblivious to his lawful obligations under the Code\n\nand is required to constantly remember his duty to the court as well as\n\nhis duty to the collective. While filing cases under section 499 and 500\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_120\"><a href=\"/doc/1459204/\" id=\"a_100\">84</a>/<a href=\"/doc/1009986/\" id=\"a_101\">153</a></span>\n\f                                                     W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_102\">IPC</a>, he is expected to maintain the independence and not act as a\n\nmachine.( Para 203)</span>\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_119\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"926207\" id=\"span_121\">e) Another aspect required to be addressed pertains to the issue of\n\nsummons. <a href=\"/doc/27007/\" id=\"a_103\">Section 199</a> Cr.P.C. envisages filing of a complaint in a court.\n\nIn case of criminal defamation, neither can any FIR be filed nor can any\n\ndirection be issued under <a href=\"/doc/99487/\" id=\"a_104\">section 156(3)</a> Cr.P.C. The offence has its own\n\ngravity and hence, the responsibility of the Magistrate is more. In a way,\n\nit is immense at the time of issue of process. Issue of process, as has\n\nbeen held in <a href=\"/doc/926207/\" id=\"a_105\">Rajendra Nath Mahato Vs T Ganguly</a> reported in 1972 1\n\nSCC 450, is a matter of judicial determination and before issuing\n\nprocess, the Magistrate has to examine the complainant.</span> <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"49712\" id=\"span_122\">In <a href=\"/doc/49712/\" id=\"a_106\">Punjab\n\nNational Bank vs Surendra Prasad Sinha</a> reported in 1993 Supp (1) SCC\n\n499, it has been held that judicial process should not be an instrument of\n\noppression or needless harassment. The court, though in a different\n\ncontext has observed that there lies responsibility and duty on the\n\nMagistracy to find whether the accused concerned should be legally\n\nresponsible for the offence charged for. Only on satisfying that the law\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_123\">85/153</span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ncasts liability or creates offence against the juristic person or the persons\n\nimpleaded, then only process should be issued. At that stage, the court\n\nwould be circumspect and judicious in exercising discretion and should\n\ntake all the relevant facts and circumstances into consideration before\n\nissuing process, lest it would be an instrument in the hands of the private\n\ncomplainant as a vendetta to harass the persons needlessly. Vindication\n\nof majesty of justice and maintenance of law and order in the society are\n\nthe prime object of criminal justice but it would not be the means to\n\nwreck personal vengeance. In Pepsi foods limited Vs Special Judicial\n\nMagistrate reported in (1998) 5 SCC 749, a two Judge bench of the\n\nSupreme Court has held that summoning of an accused in a criminal case\n\nis a serious matter and criminal law cannot be set into motion as a\n\nmatter of course. ( Para 207).</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"PetArg\" id=\"p_120\">         f) Heavy burden is on the Magistrate to scrutinize the complaint\n\nfrom all aspects. The Magistrate has also to keep in view the language\n\nemployed in <a href=\"/doc/1149595/\" id=\"a_107\">section 202</a> Cr.P.C. which stipulates about the residence of\n\nthe accused at the place beyond the area in which the Magistrate\n\nexercises his jurisdiction. He must be satisfied that ingredients of <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_108\">section\n\n499</a> Cr.P.C. are satisfied. Application of mind in the case of a private\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_124\">86/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ncomplaint is imperative.( Para 208)\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_121\">         g) It will be open to the petitioner to challenge the issue of\n\nsummons before the High Court either under <a href=\"/doc/1712542/\" id=\"a_109\">Article 226</a> of the\n\nConstitution of India or under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_110\">section 482</a> Cr.P.C. as advised and seek\n\nappropriate relief.(Para 210).\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_122\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_123\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1327342\" id=\"span_125\">27.The Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court while considering another case\n\ninvolving criminal defamation in the case of <a href=\"/doc/1327342/\" id=\"a_111\">J. Khushboo vs\n\nKanniammal and another</a> reported in 2010 5 SCC 600 held as follows:</span>\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_46\">         a) The framers of our Constitution recognized the importance of\n\nsafeguarding the freedom of speech and expression, since the free flow\n\nof opinions and ideas is essential to sustain the collective life of the\n\ncitizenry. While an informed citizenry is a precondition for meaningful\n\ngovernance in the political sense, a culture of open dialogue must also\n\nbe promoted when it comes to social attitudes. (Para 45)\n\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_47\">         b) If the complainants vehemently disagreed with the Appellants\n\nviews, then they should have contested her views through the news\n\nmedia or any other public platform. The law should not be used in a\n\nmanner that has chilling effect on the freedom of speech and expression.\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_126\">87/153</span>\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_124\">\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nDissemination of news and views for popular consumption is permissible\n\nunder our constitutional scheme. An expression of opinion in favour of\n\nnon-dogmatic and non-conventional morality has to be tolerated as the\n\nsame cannot be a ground to penalise the author. (Paragraphs 47, 50 and\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_125\">45)\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_126\">         c) There is no prima facie case of defamation in the present case.\n\nThere was neither any intent on the part of the Appellant to cause harm\n\nto the reputation of the complainants as contemplated by <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_112\">section 499</a> IPC\n\nnor can one discern any actual harm done to the reputation. In short,\n\nboth the elements of Mens Rea and Actus Reus are missing.( Paras 34 and\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_127\">35)\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_128\">         d) The limitation and the power to take cognizance of Defamation\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/27007/\" id=\"a_113\">section 199</a> Cr.P.C. serves the rational purpose of discouraging the\n\nfiling of frivolous complaints which would otherwise clog the Magistrate\n\nCourts. In the given facts of the present case, the complainants cannot\n\nbe properly described as persons aggrieved within the meaning of <a href=\"/doc/1265544/\" id=\"a_114\">section\n\n199(1)</a> Cr.P.C. as there was no specific legal injury caused to any of\n\nthem. (Para 35)\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_129\">         e) Thus, it has to be held that the institution of the numerous\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_127\">88/153</span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nCriminal complaints against the Appellant was done in malafide manner.\n\nIn order to prevent the abuse of the criminal law machinery, it would be\n\nappropriate to grant the relief sought by the Appellant. In such cases,\n\nthe proper course for Magistrates is to use their statutory powers to\n\ndirect an investigation into the allegations before taking cognizance of\n\nthe offences alleged. It is not the task of the criminal law to punish\n\nindividuals merely for expressing unpopular views. The threshold for\n\nplacing reasonable restrictions on the \u201cfreedom of speech and\n\nexpression\u201d is indeed a very high one and there should be a presumption\n\nin favour of the accused in such cases. It is only when the complainants\n\nproduce materials that support a prima facie case for a statutory offence\n\nthat the Magistrates can proceed to take cognizance of the same. (Para\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_130\">44).\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_131\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_132\">         <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1902038\" id=\"span_128\">28.The Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in paragraph 614 of its judgment in\n\nthe case of <a href=\"/doc/1902038/\" id=\"a_115\">Express id=\"a_118\">Shreya\n\nSinghal vs Union of India</a> reported in AR 2015 SC 1523 while striking\n\ndown <a href=\"/doc/170483278/\" id=\"a_119\">section 66 A </a>of the Information Technology Act on vagueness. The latest decision of the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in the case of\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1115541/\" id=\"a_123\">K.K.Mishra vs State of Madhya Pradesh</a> reported in (2018) 6 SCC 676\n\nhas cleared the doubts conclusively as to when <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_124\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. can\n\nbe invoked through a Public Prosecutor for launching prosecution for\n\ncriminal defamation.</span> The Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court followed its earlier\n\ndecision in the case of PC Joshi and another vs The State of Uttar\n\nPradesh reported in AIR 1961 SC 387 and held as follows:\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_48\">         a) <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_125\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. provides for a special procedure with\n\nregard to initiation of a prosecution for offence of defamation committed\n\nagainst the constitutional functionaries and public servants mentioned\n\ntherein. The\n\nprosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_126\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. is required to be initiated by\n\nthe public prosecutor on receipt of a previous sanction of the competent\n\nauthority in the State/Central Government under section 199(4) of the\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_135\">91/153</span>\n\f                                                      W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ncode. The core reason which the Court\n\nheld to be rationale for the special procedure engrafted by <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_130\">Section\n\n199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. is that the offence of defamation committed against the\n\nfunctionaries mentioned therein is really an offence committed against\n\nthe State as the same relate to the discharge of public functions by such\n\nfunctionaries. (\u201cNew code\u201d). <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_133\">Section 199(2)</a> and <a href=\"/doc/1901955/\" id=\"a_134\">199(4)</a> Cr.P.C. provides an\n\ninbuilt safeguard which require the public prosecutor to scan and be\n\nsatisfied with the materials on the basis of which a complaint for\n\ndefamation is to be filed by him acting as the public prosecutor. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"80997184\" id=\"span_137\">Public\n\nprosecutor filing a complaint under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_135\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C. without due\n\nsatisfaction that the materials/allegations in complaint discloses an\n\noffence against an authority or against public functionary which\n\nadversely affects the interest of the State would be abhorrent to the\n\nprinciples on the basis of which the special provision under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_136\">section 199(2)</a>\n\nand <a href=\"/doc/1901955/\" id=\"a_137\">199(4)</a> Cr.P.C. has been structured as held by this Court in PC Joshi\n\n(supra )and <a href=\"/doc/80997184/\" id=\"a_138\">Subramanian Swamy</a> (supra). The public prosecutor in terms\n\nof the statutory scheme under the <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_139\">Criminal Procedure Code</a> plays an\n\nimportant role. He is supposed to be an independent person and apply his\n\nmind to the materials placed before him\n\n\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_138\">93/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch</span>\n\n\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_138\">         33. The facts of the case in KK Mishra referred to supra was that\n\nthe accused allegedly committed offence of defamation against the Chief\n\nMinister of the State on account of certain statements with regard to the\n\nChief Minister in course of a press conference that he addressed as Chief\n\nspokesperson of a political party. The statements made by the accused\n\nare as follows:\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_51\">          \u201c19 amongst the transport inspectors appointed in\n          Madhya Pradesh are from the in-laws house Gondiya\n          (Maharashtra) of Chief Minister Shiv Raj Singh Chauhan.\n          Conversation has been made with the accused persons\n          of the Vyapam Scam from the mobile of Sanjay\n          Chauhan, son of Phoolsingh Chauhan-Mama of the Chief\n          Minister Shri Shiv Raj Singh Chauhan. The Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court held that none of the alleged\n\ndefamatory statements, in respect of which sanction was accorded to the\n\npublic prosecutor to file complaint under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_140\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C., even if\n\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_139\">94/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nadmitted to have been made by the accused can be said to have any\n\nreasonable connection with discharge of public duties by or the office of\n\nthe Chief Minister. The materials assessed shall be indicated\n\nby the Sessions Court in its order taking cognizance of the complaint filed\n\nunder section 199(2) Cr.P.C.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_71\"><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_160\">110/153</span>\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_165\">\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_166\">      h) The public prosecutor in a complaint filed under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_168\">section 199(2)</a>\n\nCr.P.C. shall be examined as a witness and only thereafter the Sessions\n\nCourt if satisfied with the materials and the complaint can take\n\ncognizance and issue process to the accused. The Proviso to <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_169\">Section 200</a>\n\nCr.P.C. as regards examination of witnesses shall be strictly followed by\n\nthe Sessions Court.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_167\">      i) The State should not be impulsive like an ordinary citizen in\n\ndefamation matters and invoke <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_170\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. to throttle\n\ndemocracy. Only in cases where there is foolproof material and when\n\nlaunching of prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_171\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. is inevitable, the\n\nsaid procedure can be invoked.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_168\">      j) Quashing of criminal complaints involving criminal defamation\n\ncan be done by the High Court exercising its power either under Article\n\n226 of the Constitution of India or under section 482 Cr.P.C.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_169\">      k) High Court has got the constitutional power to quash\n\nGovernment Orders sanctioning prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_172\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C.,\n\nif the competent authority, without any material as to how the State is\n\ndefamed, has sanctioned prosecution.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_170\">\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_161\">111/153</span>\n\f                                                    W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_171\">      49. State is like a parent for all citizens in so far as Defamation law\n\nis concerned. It is normal for some parents to face vituperative insults\n\nfrom their children. Despite those insults, parents don\u2019t disown their\n\nchildren quite easily. They always have the hope that their children will\n\nmend themselves in the near future. Only in rarest of rare cases when\n\nthe character and behaviour of their children is irretrievably broken\n\ndown and irreconcilable, the parents disown them. The attitude of the\n\nState with regard to defamation must also be the same as their tolerance\n\nlevel towards its citizens in so far as defamation is concerned must be\n\nakin to that of parents. When the state is having other avenues under law\n\nto make the offender realise the mistake if any, the criminal defamation\n\nlaw under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_173\">section 499</a> and <a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_174\">500</a> IPC should be sparingly used by the State.\n\nAn individual or a public servant/constitutional functionary can be\n\nimpulsive but not the State which will have to show utmost restraint and\n\nmaturity in filing criminal defamation cases. If the State becomes an\n\nimpulsive prosecutor in criminal defamation matters that too in an era of\n\nsocial media where there are scores of abusive contents made against\n\npublic figures, the Sessions Court will get clogged with innumerable\n\nmatters which are sometimes vindictive in nature only to settle scores\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_162\">112/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nwith opposition political parties. The intention of the legislature would\n\nnever have been for this unlawful object.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_172\">\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_173\">      50. The State must be fully convinced based on the materials that\n\nthe ingredients of criminal defamation under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_175\">section 499</a> IPC have been\n\nfully satisfied and the act committed by the alleged offender does not\n\ncome within any of the many exceptions contained in <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_176\">section 499</a> IPC.\n\nThis Court obtained from the registry of this Court statistics of the cases\n\nfiled under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_177\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. through the public prosecutor of Tamil\n\nNadu in the State of Tamilnadu from the year 2012 to 2020 and finds a\n\nslew of cases filed totally numbering 226 cases are pending on the file of\n\nvarious Sessions courts till date. Even as seen from these batch of writ\n\npetitions, within a short period, the State has filed a slew of Criminal\n\ndefamation cases. As seen from the data, irrespective of political party\n\nwho is in power, cases under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_178\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. have been filed. In\n\nmany cases, the High Court has stayed the prosecution. Due to the\n\nmechanical filing of complaints under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_179\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C., the Sessions\n\nCourts are sometimes clogged with those matters due to reckless filing\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_163\">113/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nwithout application of mind and sometimes vindictively. This menace will\n\nhave to be curbed and nipped in the bud. The Criminal defamation law is\n\nmeant for a laudable object in real cases of necessity and cannot be\n\nmisused by using the State as a tool to settle scores of a public\n\nservant/constitutional functionary over his/her adversary. A public\n\nservant/constitutional functionary must be able to face criticism. As\n\npublic servants/constitutional functionaries, they owe a solemn duty to\n\nthe people. The state cannot use criminal defamation cases to throttle\n\ndemocracy.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_174\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_175\">      51. In criminal defamation cases, police cannot register FIR under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_180\">section 499</a> IPC against the accused. Only through a complaint under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/27007/\" id=\"a_181\">section 199</a> Cr.P.C., the defamer can be prosecuted. The level of\n\nscrutiny by a Sessions Judge/Magistrate before taking cognizance of\n\ncriminal defamation cases under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_182\">section 499</a> IPC is more stringent and\n\npainstaking as there is no police report/charge sheet available as in the\n\ncase of cognizable offences where the Police Officer submits an\n\ninvestigation report under <a href=\"/doc/1867088/\" id=\"a_183\">section 173 (3)</a> of Cr.P.C. to the Learned\n\nMagistrate. The legislature has tightened the screws in so far as criminal\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_164\">114/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ndefamation cases filed through a public prosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_184\">section 199(2)</a>\n\nCr.P.C. are concerned to avoid any abuse or misuse of the said provision.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_176\">      52. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"115650339\" id=\"span_165\">Insofar as the role of the public prosecutor is concerned,\n\n<a href=\"/doc/115650339/\" id=\"a_185\">Bairam Muralidhar</a>&#x27;s case rendered by the Hon\u2019ble Supreme referred to\n\nsupra has made it clear that a public prosecutor cannot act like a mere\n\npost office but should independently apply his mind before prosecuting\n\nthe criminal complaint and he should also be fair to the court.</span>\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_177\">      53. In the considered view of this court, following are the basic\n\nrequirements of a Public Prosecutor:\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_72\">      a) A Public prosecutor must consider himself/herself as an agent of\n\njustice.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_73\">      b) There should not be on the part of the public prosecutor a blind\n\neagerness for, or grasping at a conviction.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_74\">      c) The prosecution of the accused persons has to be conducted\nwith utmost fairness. In undertaking the prosecution, the State is not\nactuated by any motives of revenge but seeks only to protect the\ncommunity. There should not therefore be seemly eagerness for, or\ngrasping at a conviction.\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_75\">\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_166\">115/153</span>\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_178\">\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_179\">      d) A public prosecutor should not by statement aggravate the case\n\nagainst the accused, or keep back a witness because his/her evidence\n\nmay weaken the case of the prosecution.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_180\">      e) A public prosecutor should place before the Court whatever\n\nevidence is in his/her possession.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_181\">      f) A public prosecutor should discharge his/her duties fairly and\n\nfearlessly and with full sense of responsibility that attaches to his/her\n\nposition.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_182\">      g) Prosecution does not mean persecution.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_183\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_184\">      54. In defamation cases filed under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_186\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C., the\n\npublic prosecutor plays a very vital role. The role is very special because\n\nin those matters, the public prosecutor plays a dual role both as a person\n\nrepresenting the public servant/constitutional functionary as well as a\n\npublic prosecutor. Therefore, the cardinal principles mentioned supra\n\nwill have to be strictly adhered to by the public prosecutor while filing\n\ncomplaints under section 199(2) Cr.P.C.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_185\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_186\">      55. The Sessions Court before taking cognizance of a complaint\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_167\">116/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nfiled under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_187\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. has to apply its judicial mind to the\n\nmaterials available on record and only if it is satisfied that ingredients\n\nfor taking cognizance under Section 199(2) has been made out, shall take\n\ncognizance and issue summons to the accused. When a specific procedure\n\nis contemplated under <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_188\">section 200</a> Cr.P.C., it cannot be deviated by\n\nadopting some other procedure which is not prescribed, even though it\n\nmay be convenient to the complainant. The purpose of recording the\n\nsubstance of sworn statement by the Magistrate/Sessions Judge is to\n\nenable the Magistrate/Sessions judge to satisfy himself of the allegation\n\nin the complaint to proceed further in the matter and also put the\n\naccused on notice about the allegations.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_187\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_188\">      56. This Court having elaborately considered and narrated the\n\ncriminal defamation law and the freedom of speech and expression\n\nprovided under <a href=\"/doc/1142233/\" id=\"a_189\">Article 19(1)</a> of the Constitution of India shall now\n\nconsider the merits of each of the writ petitions separately. In all the\n\ncases, the core ingredient required for prosecution through a public\n\nprosecutor under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_190\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. namely \u201cDefamation of the\n\nState\u201d is missing. In all the matters, while granting sanction for\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_168\">117/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nprosecution to a public prosecutor, the respective sanction orders are\n\ntotally silent as to whether the state has been defamed on account of\n\nthe alleged defamation of the public servant/constitutional functionary\n\nwhile discharging his/her public functions. It has been made crystal clear\n\nby the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in KK Mishra\u2019s case as well as in P.C\n\nJoshi\u2019s case referred to supra that prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_191\">section 199(2)</a>\n\nCr.P.C. is on account of defamation against the State.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_189\">      57. As <a href=\"/doc/115650339/\" id=\"a_192\">laid down by</a> various decisions of the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court\n\nas well as the High courts, before granting sanction for prosecution, the\n\ncompetent authority of the State shall have to apply its mind to the\n\nmaterials and should be satisfied with the same and only thereafter\n\nshould sanction prosecution. As observed earlier, in all the cases which\n\nare the subject matter of consideration by this court, the State has\n\nsanctioned prosecution in a mechanical fashion by total non application\n\nof mind as the fundamental requirement for prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_193\">section\n\n199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. namely \u201cDefamation of the State\u201d does not find a place in\n\nall the sanction orders. The public prosecutor as well as the Sessions\n\nJudge in cases where cognizance has already been taken by the Sessions\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_169\">118/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nCourt have also not applied their mind independently as the core essence\n\nof prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_194\">section 199 (2)</a> Cr.P.C. namely \u201cDefamation of the\n\nstate\u201d has not been satisfied as seen from the sanction orders. On this\n\nscore alone, all the Government Orders and the consequential complaints\n\nfor criminal defamation under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_195\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. will have to fail.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_190\">      58. The pleadings in the respective complaints also does not spell\n\nout any defamation of the State. The Public Prosecutor or any other\n\nwitness has also not been examined as a witness and given their sworn\n\nstatement before the Sessions Court which is mandatory under <a href=\"/doc/444619/\" id=\"a_196\">Section\n\n200</a> Cr.P.C. The Sessions Court has also in a mechanical fashion by total\n\nnon application of its judicial mind and without detailing the materials it\n\nhad scrutinized has taken cognizance and issued process to the accused.\n\nAll these factors will conclusively infer abuse of process of law against\n\nthe respective accused and hence, all the sanction orders as well as the\n\ncorresponding complaints filed under Section 199(2) will have to be\n\nnecessarily quashed.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_191\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_192\">      59. These batch of writ petitions involve some cases where on the\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_170\">119/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nface of it, a conclusive inference can be made that no Criminal\n\nDefamation whatsoever has been made out. However, in respect of other\n\ncases, the accused can be prosecuted for criminal defamation under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/1459845/\" id=\"a_197\">section 199(6)</a> Cr.P.C. before the Magistrate but not under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_198\">Section 199(2)</a>\n\nCr.P.C. before the Sessions Court as no \u201cDefamation against the state\u201d\n\nhas been made out or projected to by the State in its sanction orders or\n\nin the respective Complaints. As\n\nin the previous cases, there is total non-application of mind by the State,\n\nPublic Prosecutor and the Sessions court as the state has not been\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_209\">143/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ndefamed nor is there a pleading to that effect. Hence the complaint filed\n\nthrough a public prosecutor is not maintainable. Hence the Writ petition\n\nwill have to be allowed.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_221\">\n\n<span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1033637\" id=\"span_210\">(u) W.P Nos.11727 &amp; 11728 of 2013\n\n      In these cases, the Petitioner as Editor, Printer and Publisher of\n\nthe Tamil news Daily \u201cDinakaran\u201d published a press statement of Mr\n\nVijaykanth, MLA on 31.03.2013 wherein he had stated that the Chief\n\nMinister had used the film industry to reach her present status but has\n\nnow completely forgotten her past for climbing up the ladder. The\n\nPetitioner has only published the contents of the press statement given\n\nby Mr.Vijayakanth, MLA and no personal comments/imputation has been\n\nmade against the Chief Minister. <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_228\">Section 499</a> IPC makes it clear that\n\nimputation must be from the mouth of the alleged defamer. The\n\nillustrations given under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_229\">section 499</a> IPC also gives examples of personal\n\nimputations only. Further, the impugned article in the newspaper does\n\nnot by no stretch of imagination relate to the conduct of the chief\n\nMinister in the discharge of her public functions. On the face of it, the\n\narticle published by the newspaper will not come within the definition of\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_211\">144/153</span>\n\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ndefamation as defined under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_230\">section 499</a> IPC. The action initiated against\n\nthis petitioner is a clear abuse of process of law and the said action will\n\nhave to be nipped in the bud at the threshold itself. The law <a href=\"/doc/1813801/\" id=\"a_231\">laid down\n\nby</a> the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in Bhajan Lal\u2019s case on quashing of FIRs\n\ncan be applied to the case of quashing of government orders sanctioning\n\nprosecution for criminal defamation also as principles evolved in <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_232\">Bhajan\n\nLals</a> case are only to prevent abuse of process of law in Criminal\n\nprosecution. The instant case neither falls under the category of 199(2)\n\nor 199(6) <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_233\">Cr.P.C</a>. as there is no criminal defamation at all. Hence the\n\nGovernment order sanctioning prosecution and the consequent Complaint\n\nwill have to be necessarily quashed.</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_222\">\n\nRelevancy of the judgements cited by the respondents:\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_80\">      61. This court shall now consider the judgements relied upon by\n\nthe State and its relevancy one by one:\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_81\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1033637\" id=\"span_212\">a) The law <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_234\">laid down by</a> the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court in a) Prakash\n\nSingh Badal\u2019s case reported in (2007) 1 SCC 1 b) Dinesh Kumar\u2019s case\n\nreported in (2012) 1 SCC 532 and c) Ameerjan\u2019s case reported in (2007)\n\n11 SCC 273 case will not come to the assistance of the State as all those\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_213\">145/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\ncases involve offences under the <a href=\"/doc/1331755/\" id=\"a_235\">Prevention of Corruption Act</a> which is a\n\ncognizable offence and the punishment is much harsher, whereas offence\n\nfor criminal defamation is a non cognizable offence, where the\n\npunishment is lesser and further proving the offence is much difficult in\n\nview of the large number of exceptions under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_236\">section 499</a> IPC. Further in\n\nCriminal defamation cases, the police are not empowered to register an\n\nFIR and the offender can be tried only in a Private Complaint before the\n\nMagistrate or the Sessions Court as the case may be. As observed earlier\n\nby this court that in all the cases which are the subject matter of this\n\nCourts consideration, the State has not applied its mind while granting\n\nsanction for prosecution as even as per their respective sanction orders,\n\nthere is no reference to any Defamation of the State which is the essence\n\nof any Defamation Complaint filed under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_237\">Section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C. The\n\nultimate test as to whether Sanction orders can be interfered with at this\n\nstage is to see whether the allegations have any substance for\n\nprosecution under Section 199(2), which in the considered view of this\n\nCourt does not have any as there is no allegation in the sanctioning\n\norders that the State has been defamed.</span>\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_82\">\n\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_214\">146/153</span>\n</blockquote><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_223\">\f                                                   W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_224\">      b) Mansukhlal Vithaldas Chauhan\u2019case reported in (1997) 7 SCC 62\n\nSCC 273 supports the case of the Petitioners and not the State. The\n\nHon\u2019ble Supreme Court held that that the validity of sanction under\n\n<a href=\"/doc/12704/\" id=\"a_238\">section 197</a> Cr.P.C depends on applicability of mind by the sanctioning\n\nauthority. Infact <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_239\">in that case</a> ,the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court quashed the\n\nsanction order for non application of mind by the Sanctioning authority.In\n\nthe cases on hand when there is no allegation that the State has been\n\ndefamed, there is no question of prosecution under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_240\">section 199(2)</a> Cr.P.C\n\nand therefore there is total non application of mind by the sanctioning\n\nauthority and this court is empowered to interfere at this stage.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_225\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1033637\" id=\"span_215\">c) Municipal Council, Neemuch\u2019s case reported in (2019) 10 SCC\n\n738 has no relevance for the instant cases as that was a case involving\n\nadministrative orders not arising out of any mandatory statutory\n\nrequirement whereas the sanction orders for prosecution is borne out of\n\nmandatory statutory requirement under <a href=\"/doc/1901955/\" id=\"a_241\">Section 199(4)</a> Cr.P.C. In\n\nNeemuch\u2019s case, the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court was dealing with sanction\n\nunder the Municipal Corporation (Transfer of Immovable Property) Rules\n\n1994 which are internal rules to help in governing the principal law.\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_216\">147/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nWhereas the mandatory requirement of sanction under Section 199(4) is\n\nfound in the principal enactment itself namely the <a href=\"/doc/445276/\" id=\"a_242\">Criminal Procedure\n\nCode</a>. Even if the principles <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_243\">laid down in</a> Neemuch\u2019s case is applied, the\n\ninstant sanction orders will have to be quashed as there is total non\n\napplication of mind by the State as without any allegation of any\n\ndefamation of the State, sanction orders have been issued.</span>\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_226\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1033637\" id=\"span_217\"><a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_244\">d) Bhajan Lal</a>&#x27;s case reported in 1992 Supp 1 SCC 335 infact\n\nsupports the case of the Petitioners. The cases on hand are fit cases for\n\nuse of the extraordinary power under <a href=\"/doc/1712542/\" id=\"a_245\">Article 226</a> or the inherent power\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_246\">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C as <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_247\">laid down by</a> the Hon\u2019ble Supreme Court as\n\nthe respective criminal prosecutions have been instituted under <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_248\">Section\n\n199(2)</a> Cr.P.C without any allegation that the state has been defamed\n\nwhich is the essence of criminal defamation complaint filed through a\n\npublic prosecutor. When on the face of it, no ground for prosecution\n\nunder section 199(2) has been made out, the sanction orders and the\n\nconsequent complaints will have to be necessarily quashed.</span>\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_227\">      62. Before Parting, after momentarily removing my cloak as a\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_218\">148/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nJudge of this Court and as an ordinary citizen of this great country, I\n\nwould like to remind the Media the great role they play in nation\n\nbuilding. The media is considered as the fourth pillar of Democracy and\n\nthey are infact the watchdog of any democracy. By their truthful and\n\nhonest reporting, they stand as pillars for building one of the respected\n\nand successful democracies of the World. Our nation has always\n\nrespected the role of the media and has highest regard for their\n\nindependent and truthful reporting. But of late for quite number of\n\nyears, there seems to be some decay happening in every sphere of\n\ndemocracy including the Media. If the rottenness is not removed sooner\n\nthan later, it will spread like fire causing great peril to our robust\n\nDemocracy.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_228\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_229\">      63. Our National Motto is \u201cSatyameva Jayate\u201d which means \u201cTruth\n\nalone triumphs\u201d. We respect the National Anthem, National Flag,\n\nNational Emblem etc., but we sometimes forget to respect the National\n\nMotto which is also equally important like others for the survival of our\n\ndemocracy. I am confident all media houses will take this humble request\n\nfrom an ordinary citizen in the right spirit and carry it forward in the\n\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_219\">149/153</span>\n\f                                                  W.P.No.5129 of 2012 etc., batch\n\n\nbest interest of this great nation. Mahatma Gandhi said \u201cthe sole aim of\n\njournalism should be service. The newspaper is a great power, but just as\n\nan unchained torrent of water submerges the whole countryside and\n\ndevastates crops, even so an uncontrolled pen serves but to destroy. If\n\nthe control is from without, it proves more poisonous than want of\n\ncontrol. It can be profitable only when exercised from within.\u201d The\n\nIndian media is highly respected and this is the reason why the nation\n\nupto now has thought it fit not to regulate the contents of reporting by\n\nimposing harsh regulations. I am confident that the media will take a cue\n\nfrom Mahatma Gandhi&#x27;s advise and introspect from within and help in\n\nnation building for the betterment of the generations to come.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_230\">      64. For the foregoing reasons, all the writ petitions deserve to be\n\nallowed as none of the prosecutions fall under the category of <a href=\"/doc/822867/\" id=\"a_249\">Section\n\n199(2)</a> Cr.P.C though some as indicated in this common order may fall\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1459845/\" id=\"a_250\">Section 199(6)</a> Cr.P.C. Accordingly, these writ petitions are\n\nallowed as prayed for. No costs.
