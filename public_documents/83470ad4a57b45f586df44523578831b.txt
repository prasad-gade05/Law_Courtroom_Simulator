The law is also well-settled that the jurisdiction to quash a\ncomplaint, F.I.R. or a charge-sheet should be exercised sparingly and\nonly in exceptional cases and Courts should not ordinarily interfere\nwith the investigations of cognizable offences. However, the law is\nequally settled that where the allegations made in the F.I.R. or the\ncomplaint even if taken at their face value and accepted in their\nentirety do not prima facie constitute any offence or make out a case\nagainst the accused, the F.I.R. or complaint may be quashed in\nexercise of powers under <a href=\"/doc/1712542/\" id=\"a_40\">Article 226</a> or inherent powers under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_41\">Section\n482</a> of the Cr.P.C. In the leading case of <a href=\"/doc/1033637/\" id=\"a_42\">\u201eState of Haryana v.\nBhajan Lal &amp; Ors.</a>&#x27;, 1992 Supp. (1) SCC 335 certain guidelines were\nissued for the exercise of these powers by the Courts. In guideline\nnumber 3 it was laid down that where the uncontroverted allegations\nmade in the F.I.R. or complaint and the evidence collected in support\nof the same do not disclose the commission of any offence and do not\nmake out a case against the accused, the Court may quash the F.I.R. as\nwell as the investigations. A note of caution was added by observing\nthat the power of quashing a criminal proceeding should be exercised\nsparingly and with circumspection and that too in the rarest of rare\ncases. It was held that the Court would not be justified in embarking\n\nCrl.M.C. No. 447/2013                                        P age 10 of 14\n upon an inquiry as to the reliability or genuineness or otherwise of the\nallegations made in the F.I.R. or the complaint.</span> <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"579822\" id=\"span_11\">Similarly, in <a href=\"/doc/579822/\" id=\"a_43\">\u201eRupan\nDeol Bajaj v. State of NCT of Delhi\u201f, (1999) 3 SCC 259, the Hon\u201fble Supreme\nCourt clearly held that if a prima facie case is made out disclosing the\ningredients      of     the   offence,   Court   should   not   quash     the\ncomplaint. Every High Court has inherent\n         power to act ex debito justitiae to do real and\n         substantial justice, for the administration of which alone\n         it exists, or to prevent abuse of the process of the court.\n         Inherent power under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_50\">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. can be\n         exercised:\n</blockquote>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_19\">         (i) to give effect to an order under the Code;\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_20\">         (ii) to prevent abuse of the process of court, and\n</blockquote><blockquote id=\"blockquote_21\">         (iii) to otherwise secure the ends of justice.\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_27\">         24. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Neutral\" data-docid=\"855018\" id=\"span_14\">Inherent powers under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_51\">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. though\n         wide have to be exercised sparingly, carefully and with\n         great caution and only when such exercise is justified\n         by the tests specifically <a href=\"/doc/855018/\" id=\"a_52\">laid down in</a> this section itself.\n         Authority of the court exists for the advancement of\n         justice. If any abuse of the process leading to injustice\n         is brought to the notice of the court, then the Court\n         would be justified in preventing injustice by invoking\n         inherent powers in absence of specific provisions in the\n         statute.&quot;</span>\n</p>\n\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_28\">25.    From the perusal of the records and the complaint of respondent\nNo. 1 it is observed that the averments made by the petitioner do\nprima facie appear to be defamatory and injurious to the image of\n\nCrl.M.C. No. 447/2013                                            P age 12 of 14\n respondent No. 1. The said imputations are directed towards the\ncharacter of the said respondent. Thus, at this stage this Court is not\nconvinced with the arguments of the petitioner that not even a prima\nfacie case is established against the petitioners for the offence alleged.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_29\">26.    It has been contended by the learned counsel for the petitioners\nthat the alleged defamatory statements made by the accused persons in\nthe pleadings of previous civil proceedings and statements under\n<a href=\"/doc/447673/\" id=\"a_53\">Section 161</a> Cr.P.C. cannot furnish a foundation for their prosecution\nfor defamation in the instant case. This contention does not find favour\nwith this court. The pleadings form a part of the judicial records and\nform a part of public documents. Anything stated in such pleadings,\ntherefore, amount to the publication of the defamatory statements.\nFurther the statements made under <a href=\"/doc/447673/\" id=\"a_54\">Section 161</a> Cr.P.C. are only\nexempted for usage at any inquiry or trial in respect of any offence\nunder investigation at the time when statement was made. So far as the\nuse of such statement made in a separate proceeding for prosecution of\nan offence under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_55\">Sections 499</a>/<a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_56\">500</a> IPC is concerned, the bar of\n<a href=\"/doc/523607/\" id=\"a_57\">Section 162</a> Cr.P.C. would not be attracted. Statements under <a href=\"/doc/447673/\" id=\"a_58\">section\n161</a> Cr.P.C. can claim only &quot;qualified privilege&quot; and not &quot;absolute\nprivilege&quot;.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_30\">27.    The other point for consideration in the present case is whether\nsuch statements are covered by the exceptions provided under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_59\">Section\n499</a> IPC. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1946272\" id=\"span_15\">In the opinion of this court the question whether or not such\nstatements are covered under the said exceptions or whether such\nstatements have been made in good faith or not cannot be decided at a\npreliminary stage and could only be determined at the time of hearing\nand not under a proceeding under <a href=\"/doc/1679850/\" id=\"a_60\">Section 482</a> Cr.P.C. In &#x27;Balraj\n\nCrl.M.C. No. 447/2013                                           P age 13 of 14\n <a href=\"/doc/1946272/\" id=\"a_61\">Khanna and Ors v. Moti Ram</a>&#x27;, AIR 1971 SC 1389 the Apex Court\nin this regard was of the view that:</span>\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_22\">       &quot;30. ...It is needless to state that the question of\n       applicability of the Exceptions to <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_62\">Section 499</a>, I.P.C, as\n       well as all other defences that may be available to the\n       appellants will have to be gone into during the trial of the\n       complaint.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_31\">28.
