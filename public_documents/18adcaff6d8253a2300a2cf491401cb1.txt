The degree of proof that is required in a criminal case, when it is\n\nfor the prosecution/complainant to prove the case against the accused, she\n\nhas to prove the guilt of the accused beyond reasonable doubt. It contains 10\n\nexceptions and 4 explanations. The relevant portion reads;\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_5\">                  &quot;<a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_11\">Section 499</a>. Defamation.-- Whoever, by words either\n                  spoken or intended to be read, or by signs or by visible\n                  representations,   makes    or   publishes   any   imputation\n                  concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing or\n                  having reason to believe that such imputation will harm,\n                  the reputation of such person, is said, except in the cases\n                  hereinafter expected, to defame that person.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_30\">     19.   Therefore, if the accused are the persons who either made or\n\npublished the defamatory imputation, they would be liable for punishment\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_12\">Section 500</a> IPC. In order to constitute an offence under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_13\">section 499</a> of\n\nIPC, the complainant needs to prove that, 1) an imputation was made\n\nconsisting of words spoken or written or intended to be read or made by signs\n\nor by visible representations; 2) the imputation concerned the complainant; 3)\n\naccused made or published incriminating imputation; and 4) that intention\n\nbehind the making or publishing the imputation caused harm to the\n\nreputation of such person.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_31\">     20.   Therefore, to constitute an offence of defamation under <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_14\">Section\n\n499</a>, there has to be imputation and it must have been made in the manner as\n\nprovided in the provision with the intention of causing harm or having reason\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_14\">                                           13</span>\n\nto believe that such imputation will harm the reputation of the person about\n\nwhom it is made. Whether the content of the Ex.P1 and Ex.P2 public notices\n\nare defamatory or not, or whether it was published at the instance of the\n\naccused, or whether the complainant&#x27;s reputation was demeaned in the\n\nestimation by others or not, are questions of facts to be determined in this\n\ncase.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_32\">        21. Ltd,\n              A CHR Enterprises,\n              507, B &amp; C 5th Floor, Kakad Chambers,\n              132- Dr.Annie Besant Road,\n              Worli, Mumbai - 400 018.&quot;\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_35\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_36\">     22.    Therefore, what is stated in the said public notice is that 1) the\n\ncomplainant has represented herself to be connected with the company,\n\nConsindia HR Services Ltd or CHR Global HR Services Pvt., Ltd., and 2) that\n\nshe is using the official email address of the company without due\n\nauthoriation and 3) that the company is concerned with the activities of AJ\n\nIshwar Kumar and his wife, i.e. the complainant.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Facts\" id=\"p_37\">     23.    There is no dispute that Sri. AJ Ishwar Kumar, husband of the\n\ncomplainant was working with M/s. Ab Initio HR Solutions Pvt Ltd., and\n\njoined the company in June 2005 as per Ex.D2, letter of Intent. In the said\n\nEx.D2, worded as offer letter, it is mentioned that he shall be getting retainer\n\nfees and that he shall be reporting to accused no.3, the Managing Director of\n\nthe Company. It is also not in dispute that Sri. AJ Ishwar Kumar was\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_16\">                                          15</span>\n\nterminated from service as per Ex.D3 letter. The said letter was addressed by\n\naccused no.1 to the husband of the complainant on 25.01.2006 stating that\n\nhis services were terminated with immediate effect as he committed various\n\noffences causing huge loss to the company. Thereafter the impugned public\n\nnotices were published as per Ex.P2 and Ex.P3 on 31.01.2006.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Facts\" id=\"p_38\">     24.   Complainant submitted that, when her husband joined the\n\norganization of the accused, she was also asked to help their organization and\n\nthat she would be compensated for her work and accordingly gave her the\n\nemail ID, &#x27;sundariishwar@abinitiopeople.com&#x27; and later, it was changed to\n\n&#x27;sundariishwar@chrglobal.co.in&#x27; with the change in the name of the company.\n\nAs per the public notices, complainant was using the email ID unauthorizedly.\n\nHowever,   complainant    has   produced      Ex.P4,   excerpt    of   an      email\n\ncorrespondence    dated   16.10.2015    received   between       the   email     ids\n\n&#x27;anjana@abinitiopeople.com&#x27; and &#x27;sundariishwar@abinitiopeople.com&#x27; where\n\nMs. Anasuya congratulated the complainant on becoming part of M/s. Ab\n\nInitio HR Solutions Pvt. Ltd and complainant stated that she learnt a lot from\n\nMs. Anjana when she was in Bangalore and also asked for her inputs and both\n\nof them exchanged pleasantries in the said email correspondence. Therefore,\n\nEx.P4 clearly shows that the company knew that the complainant was allotted\n\nwith an official emial ID and the same was being used by her.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_39\">     25.   Complainant also produced Ex.P5 email correspondence dated\n\n5.12.2005 where Mr. Hemant Ojha had requested Ms. Anusuya of the said\n\nCompany to give him the list of emails whose names had to be inserted in to\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_17\">                                          16</span>\n\nthe new domain, &#x27;@chrglobal.co.in&#x27; list. Accordingly, Ms.Anusuya replied to the\n\nsaid email at 1.33 pm as per Ex.P6, giving the list of existing email IDs\n\nattached to the domain &#x27;@abinitiopeople.com&#x27;, so that the same may be\n\nincluded in the new domain &#x27;@chrglobal.co.in&#x27;. Further, as per Ex.P7, on\n\n5.12.2005, Hemant Obja sent an email to Anasuya and Ishwar Kumar at 6.25\n\npm    mentioning     the    new     email     IDs,   which    also    included\n\n&#x27;sundariishwar@chrglobal.co.on&#x27;. Complainant also produced Ex.P8 email\n\nexcerpt sent by Assistant Vice President, Rajib Sarkar, saying goodbye to his\n\ncompany, which was also marked to the complainant at her email ID,\n\n&#x27;sundariishwar@chrglobal.co.in&#x27;.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_40\">     26.    Accused contended that no email was sent to her directly from the\n\naccused persons creating her corporate email ID. It is also argued that when\n\nthe email ID was created, complainant&#x27;s husband was associated with the\n\naccused company&#x27;s Branch Office at Bengaluru and it is under his\n\ninstructions the email ID was created, without the knowledge of the accused.\n\nIt is also argued that on account of unauthorized activities from her mail ID\n\nwithout authorization from the company, her husband was terminated by the\n\naccused. However, accused has not produced any document to show that due\n\nto the activities in the email account of the complainant, her husband, AJ\n\nIshwar Kumar was terminated from service.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_41\">     27.    Complainant in fact submitted that, they had given a complaint to\n\nthe Cyber Crime Police Station and the Cyber Crime Police got the information\n\nfrom Net4 India that the email ID of the complainant was created by the\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_18\">                                              17</span>\n\ncompany itself. She produced Ex.P9, letter of Net4 India addressed to Cyber\n\nCrime Police Station on 09.03.2006 mentioning the details of the domain and\n\nthat the domain, @abinitiopeople.com belonged to Consindia HR Services Pvt.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_42\">Ltd        and       the     Admin&#x27;s      and     Tech&#x27;s      email     ID     was\n\nmuditranjan@consindiaglobal.com and the same was operated from Vasant\n\nVihar in Delhi. Based on the said report of Net4 India, Cyber Crime Police\n\nissued a letter on 22.03.2006 that the email ID of the complainant was\n\nconfigured officially and allotted to the complainant. Therefore, accused\n\ncannot argue that the email ID was created at the instance of her husband\n\nsince the email ID was created by the Admin in Delhi and thereafter, the same\n\nis acknowledged by the employees of the Company. Hence, in this background,\n\non perusal of the Ex.P2 and Ex.P3 public notice issued by M/s. Ab initio HR\n\nSolutions Pvt Ltd, Mumbai, the same amounts to defamatation.\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_43\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1120728\" id=\"span_19\">28.        As per <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_15\">section 499</a> of IPC, except in the nine circumstances\n\nmentioned as exceptions, whoever makes or publishes any imputations\n\nconcerning any person intending to harm the reputation, he is said to defame\n\nthat person. The Apex Court in the decision of <a href=\"/doc/1120728/\" id=\"a_16\">M.C. Vaerghese v. T.J. Ponnan\n\nand another</a>, AIR 1970 SC 1876, observed that the exceptions to <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_17\">section 499</a>\n\nmust be regarded as exhaustive as to the cases which they purport to cover\n\nand recourse cannot be had to add new grounds to the exceptions, beyond the\n\nstatute.</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_44\">      29.        Accused took up a specific defence that what is published in Ex.P2\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_20\">                                            18</span>\n\nand Ex.P3 is the truth and the same was published for the public good as a\n\npublic warning. It is undoubtedly true that the first Exception exempts from\n\ncriminal liability, statements which are true and which are required for public\n\ngood. The first exception to <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_18\">section 499</a> of IPC which reads as follows:\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_10\">            &quot;First Exception.--Imputation of truth which public good requires\n            to be made or published: It is not defamation to impute anything\n            which is true concerning any person, if it be for the public good\n            that the imputation should be made or published. Whether or not\n            it is for the public good is a question of fact.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n\n<p data-structure=\"Section\" id=\"p_45\">      30.   A bare reading of the first exception reveals that <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_19\">section 499</a> of IPC\n\ncontemplates truth as a defense and to operate as a valid defense in a criminal\n\ncase for defamation. Exceptions one postulates that if the imputation is true, it\n\nis not defamation. Because to import anything which is true concerning any\n\nperson and for the public good, it is not defamation to the person against\n\nwhom such imputation is made or published. However, it is for the accused\n\nwho has published or made the imputation, to prove the same to be true,\n\nwithout which he is not an entitled to the benefit of first exception.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_46\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"1226845\" id=\"span_21\">31.   The Apex Court in <a href=\"/doc/1226845/\" id=\"a_20\">Chaman Lal v. The State of Punjab</a>, 1970 Cri LJ\n\n1266, observed that, in order to come within the First exception to <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_21\">section 499</a>\n\nof the IPC, it has to be established that what has been imputed concerning\n\naccused is true and the publication of the imputation is for the public good.\n\nThe onus of proving these two ingredients, namely, truth of the imputation\n\nand the publication of the imputation for the public good is on the\n\ncomplainant.</span>\n</p><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_22\">                                            19</span><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_47\">\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_48\">     32.    Accused no.3 submitted that what was published was the truth\n\nand for the public good. However from the perusal of Ex.P4 to Ex.P7 email\n\ncorrespondences and the Ex.P8 report, it cannot be true that the email ID,\n\nsundariishwar@abinitiopeople.com      or   sundariishwar@chrglobal.co.in        was\n\ncreated unauthorizedly and that the company was not aware that the\n\ncomplainant was using the said email IDs. Accused no.3 has not produced any\n\ndocument to show that how the email ID was being misused by the\n\ncomplainant or her husband. Therefore, accused no.3 has failed to justify that\n\nwhat was published as per Ex.P2 and Ex.P3, is the truth and the same is\n\npublished in good faith.\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_49\">     33.    Therefore, complainant proves that an imputation published in\n\nTimes of India and Economic Times dated 31.01.2006 by the M/s.Ab Intitio\n\nHR Solutions Ltd., was with an intention and knowledge that such imputation\n\nwould cause harm to the reputation of the complainant.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_50\">            Question No.2: Whether the accused no.3 is responsible\n            and liable for the said publication?\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_51\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_52\">     34.    From the above discussion, what is proved by the complainant is\n\nthat the company, M/s. Ab Intitio HR Solutions Ltd., having its office at Worli,\n\nMumbai got the said public notices published. However, the said company has\n\nnot been arraigned as an accused in this case. Therefore, the complaint\n\nwithout making the company, M/s.Ab Intitio HR Solutions Pvt Ltd., a party is\n\nnot even maintainable as there can be no vicarious liability in <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_22\">IPC</a> offences.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_53\">     35.    Further, accused no.3 is said to be the Managing Director of M/s.\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_23\">                                            20</span>\n\nAb Intitio HR Solutions Pvt, to whom, complainant&#x27;s husband, AJ Ishwar\n\nKumar reported to. Ex.D2 document marked from the accused&#x27;s side shows\n\nthat accused no.3 was the Managing Director of the said company at the time\n\nwhen the husband of the complainant joined the organization. Thereafter, no\n\ndocument is produced to show if accused no.3 was still the Managing Director\n\nof the organization even on 25.01.2006, when complainant&#x27;s husband was\n\nterminated from service. Moreover, on perusal of Ex.D1 and Ex.D3, the\n\ncorrespondence is with the accused no.1 and there is nothing on record to\n\nshow that accused no.3 was responsible for the said publications.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_54\">      <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"13980764\" id=\"span_24\">36.   The Hon&#x27;ble High Court of Karnataka in a similar case in <a href=\"/doc/13980764/\" id=\"a_23\">Rajeev\n\nChandrasekhar     v.   K.   Koteswar   Rao,     Crl</a>.   Pet.No.101127/2015   dated\n\n17.02.2022, took note of the Apex Court decisions and observed that: &quot;The\n\npetitioner could not have been roped in for having committed the offence\n\nunder <a href=\"/doc/1041742/\" id=\"a_24\">Sections 499</a> and <a href=\"/doc/1408202/\" id=\"a_25\">500</a> of the IPC. 12. As held by the Apex Court, the\n\nprinciple of vicarious liability is not applicable to criminal offences in the\n\nabsence of any provision <a href=\"/doc/13980764/\" id=\"a_26\">laid down in</a> the statute. The statute applicable in the\n\ncase at hand is the Act or the <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_27\">IPC</a>. The Managing Director thus cannot be held\n\nto be vicariously liable for the acts committed by the Company or its\n\nemployees merely because he happens to be the Managing Director of the TV\n\nnews channel.&quot;</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_55\">      37.   Therefore, when a complainant intends to rope in a Managing\n\nDirector or any officer of a company, it is essential to make requisite allegation\n\nagainst him, since there is no concept of vicarious liability under <a href=\"/doc/1569253/\" id=\"a_28\">IPC</a>. In this\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_25\">                                                 21</span>\n\ncase, there is no evidence to show that accused no.3 was privy to the\n\npublication of public notices in the two newspapers or that he was directly\n\nresponsible for the said publications. Further, the very complaint is also not\n\nmaintainable as it has not arraigned the company, M/s. Ab Intitio HR\n\nSolutions Pvt Ltd., under whose name the said public notices were published.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_56\">      38.     Therefore, the burden of proof on the complainant being that of\n\nbeyond reasonable doubt, she has failed to prove that the accused no.3 was\n\nresponsible for the said publications. As such, she has failed to prove that\n\naccused no.3 defamed her. Accordingly, I answer Points No.1 in the Negative.\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_57\">      39.
