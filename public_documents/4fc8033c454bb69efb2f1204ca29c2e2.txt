It cannot be the rule and the question of its\n            grant should be left to the cautious and judicious\n            discretion of the Court depending on the facts and\n            circumstances of each case. While called upon to exercise\n            the said power, the Court concerned has to be very\n            cautious as the grant of interim protection or protection\n            to the accused in serious cases may lead to a miscarriage\n            of justice and may hamper the investigation to a great\n            extent as it may sometimes lead to tampering or\n            distraction of the evidence. We shall not be understood to\n            have held that the Court shall not pass interim protection\n            pending consideration of such application as the Section\n            is destined to safeguard the freedom of an individual\n            against unwarranted arrest and we say that such orders\n            shall be passed in eminently fit cases.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_15\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"73887298\" id=\"span_9\">9.          It was held in <a href=\"/doc/73887298/\" id=\"a_5\">Pratibha Manchanda v. State of Haryana</a>,\n\n(2023) 8 SCC 181: 2023 SCC OnLine SC 785 that the Courts should\n\nbalance individual rights, public interest and fair investigation\n\nwhile considering an application for pre-arrest bail. It was\n\nobserved:</span>\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_3\">            &quot;21. The relief of anticipatory bail is aimed at\n            safeguarding individual rights. While it serves as a crucial\n            tool to prevent the misuse of the power of arrest and\n            protects innocent individuals from harassment, it also\n            presents challenges in maintaining a delicate balance\n            between individual rights and the interests of justice. The\n            tightrope we must walk lies in striking a balance between\n            safeguarding individual rights and protecting public\n            interest. While the right to liberty and presumption of\n            innocence are vital, the court must also consider the\n            gravity of the offence, the impact on society, and the need\n            for a fair and free investigation. The court&#x27;s discretion in\n            weighing these interests in the facts and circumstances of\n            each case becomes crucial to ensure a just outcome.&quot;\n</blockquote><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_10\">                               7</span>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_16\">                                                             2024:HHC:12715\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_17\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"34374887\" id=\"span_11\">10.         It   is   undisputed   that   bail   petitions   filed     by\n\nMohammad Kasim and Mohammad Asif were dismissed by this\n\nCourt because the source of the video was to be verified and the\n\ndetails of the persons to whom the video was sent were also to\n\nbe verified. The bail application was also dismissed by the\n\nHon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in SLP (Crl). No. 13994 of 2024 titled\n\n<a href=\"/doc/34374887/\" id=\"a_6\">Mohammad Kasim vs. State of H.P</a>.</span>\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"CDiscource\" id=\"p_18\">11.        It is also undisputed that the petitioner had also\n\nreceived and forwarded the video. She claimed that it was done\n\nat the instance of the victim but there is no proof of this fact.\n\nFurther, it is not explained why the video should have been\n\nforwarded to her cousin and not to the victim. FIR reads that the\n\nco-accused tried to hack the social media account. It does not\n\nmention that the account was really hacked. No detail of the\n\nhacked social media account was given; hence, the explanation\n\nprovided by the petitioner that the victim&#x27;s social media account\n\nwas hacked is no explanation at all. The role of the petitioner\n\ncannot be distinguished from the other co-accused. She claimed\n\nto have received a video from Pooja, but the latter claimed that\n\nshe had not sent the video to the petitioner. This fact is also to be\n\nverified. <span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"34374887\" id=\"span_12\">The source of the video is to be ascertained. The status\n<span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_13\">                                8</span>\n                                                            2024:HHC:12715\n\n\n\n\nreport shows that the petitioner had not joined the investigation\n\nand when she was called, she switched off her mobile phone.\n\nThis shows that the plea of the police that custodial\n\ninterrogation of the petitioner is required is acceptable. It was\n\n<a href=\"/doc/34374887/\" id=\"a_7\">laid down by</a> the Hon&#x27;ble Supreme Court in State Versus Anil\n\nSharma (1997) 7 SCC 187 that where custodial interrogation is\n\nrequired, pre-arrest bail should not be granted.               It was\n\nobserved:-</span>\n</p>\n<blockquote id=\"blockquote_4\">     &quot;6. We find force in the submission of the CBI that custodial\n     interrogation is qualitatively more elicitation-orientated than\n     questioning a suspect who is well-ensconced with a favourable\n     order under Section 438 of the Code. In a case like this effective\n     interrogation of a suspected person is of tremendous advantage\n     in disinterring many useful information and also materials\n     which would have been concealed. Success in such interrogation\n     would elude if the suspected person knows that he is well\n     protected and insulated by a pre-arrest bail order during the\n     time he is interrogated. Very often interrogation in such a\n     condition would reduce to a mere ritual. The argument that the\n     custodial interrogation is fraught with the danger of the person\n     being subjected to third-degree methods need not be\n     countenanced, for, such an argument can be advanced by all\n     accused in all criminal cases. The Court has to presume that\n     responsible Police Officers would conduct themselves in a\n     responsible manner and that those entrusted with the task of\n     disinterring offences would not conduct themselves as\n     offender&quot;\n</blockquote><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_14\">                                     9</span>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_19\">                                                                  2024:HHC:12715\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_20\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"68600001\" id=\"span_15\">12.        A similar view was taken by the Delhi High Court in <a href=\"/doc/68600001/\" id=\"a_8\">Mukesh\n\nKhurana v. State (NCT of Delhi</a>), 2022 SCC OnLine Del 1032 wherein it\n\nwas observed: -</span>\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_21\">           &quot;13. One of the significant factors in determining this question\n           would be the need for custodial interrogation. Without a doubt,\n           custodial interrogation is more effective to question a suspect.\n           The cocoon of protection, afforded by a bail order insulates the\n           suspect and he could thwart interrogation reducing it to futile\n           rituals. But it must be also kept in mind, that while\n           interrogation of a suspect is one of the basic and effective\n           methods of crime solving, the liberty of an individual also needs\n           to be balanced out.&quot;\n</p>\n<p data-structure=\"Precedent\" id=\"p_22\"><span class=\"citetext\" data-sentiment=\"Pos\" data-docid=\"90251163\" id=\"span_16\">13.        It was held in <a href=\"/doc/90251163/\" id=\"a_9\">P Chidambaram</a> (supra) that the grant of\n\npre-arrest bail may hamper the investigation. It was observed:</span>\n\n</p><blockquote id=\"blockquote_5\">           &quot;83. Grant of anticipatory bail at the stage of investigation may\n           frustrate the investigating agency in interrogating the accused\n           and in collecting useful information and also the materials\n           which might have been concealed. Success in such interrogation\n           would elude if the accused knew that he is protected by the\n           order of the court. Grant of anticipatory bail, particularly in\n           economic offences would definitely hamper the effective\n           investigation. Having regard to the materials said to have been\n           collected by the respondent Enforcement Directorate and\n           considering the stage of the investigation, we are of the view\n           that it is not a fit case to grant anticipatory bail.&quot;\n</blockquote>\n<p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_23\">14.        In view of the above, the petitioner does not deserve the\n\nconcession of pre-arrest bail; hence, the present petition fails and the\n\nsame is dismissed.\n</p><span class=\"hidden_text\" id=\"span_17\">                                  10</span><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_24\">\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_25\">                                                            2024:HHC:12715\n\n\n\n\n</p><p data-structure=\"Conclusion\" id=\"p_26\">15.
